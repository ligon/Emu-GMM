* Section 1: Scope and Sequencing

This plan operationalises *Path A* of the pymanopt-integration memo
([[file:design-notes/pymanopt-integration.org][docs/design-notes/pymanopt-integration.org]], PR #27) as a sequence of
phases that extend v1 ([[file:implementation-plan.org][docs/implementation-plan.org]], Phases 1--8 DONE).
The architectural mandate from the memo: JAX-native implementations of
=Euclidean=, =PSDFixedRank=, =Product= plus a Riemannian Gauss--Newton
NLLS solver, with an optional =from_pymanopt(M)= adapter for exotic
manifolds. K-Aggregators's =Product(PSDFixedRank(m, K), Euclidean(...),
Euclidean(...))= is the concrete v2 acceptance target.

Sequencing:

1. /Manifold foundations/ --- the type machinery (=ManifoldParam=
   protocol, =ManifoldSpec= type, =ManifoldLeaf= PyTree node, relaxed
   PyTree contract) plus the trivial =Euclidean= reference and the
   =tangent_basis_names= function. Reproduces v1 behaviour on the
   existing acceptance tests; no inference changes yet.
2. /PSDFixedRank/ --- the hard one. Numerical-equivalence tests
   against pymanopt's NumPy implementation, including the
   Lyapunov-solve AD adjoint.
3. /Product manifold/ --- composition; per-factor dispatch.
4. /Optimiser surface/ --- =RiemannianOptimizer= protocol and
   =RiemannianLM=; =OptimistixManifoldAdapter= so the v1 API still
   works for users with all-Euclidean parameter PyTrees.
5. /Estimator + Jacobian/ --- manifold-aware unflatten;
   protocol-version dispatch; Riemannian info matrix; gauge-aware
   condition number; =J_dof= contract change.
6. /Result + Diagnostics/ --- =theta_hat= carries manifold structure;
   =.components()= accessor (K-Agg idiom); tangent-space
   =Sigma_theta=; =gauge_nullspace_dim=; =Sigma_theta_basis=;
   spectral-gap diagnostic.
7. /Optional pymanopt adapter/ --- =from_pymanopt(M)= under the
   =[manifold]= extra; advertised non-JIT, advertised
   bootstrap/vmap caveat.
8. /Polish/ --- v2 quickstart, CLAUDE.md update, deferrals refresh,
   companion mcar-asymptotics-riemannian note.

Each phase has dependencies, tasks, and an acceptance criterion. The
first non-trivial end-to-end run is at the end of Phase 5
(=PSDFixedRank= recovery via =RiemannianLM=). The full K-Aggregators
target shape (=Product(PSDFixedRank, Euclidean, Euclidean)=) lights up
at the end of Phase 6.

Effort estimates follow the v1 convention: /S/ ≈ 1--2 hours, /M/ ≈
half-day, /L/ ≈ full day, /XL/ ≈ multi-day. Calibrated against
/Sue + Claude pair-of-hours/, not solo developer time. The v2 estimates
have been recalibrated (several items moved from L to XL) per the
plan-fidelity review.

** §2.1 Storage convention on =PSDFixedRank= --- DECIDED: ambient-shape with gauge bookkeeping

Pymanopt's =PSDFixedRank(n, k)= (=psd.py:7-70=) parameterises
\(X = Y Y^\top\) with \(Y \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times k}\), identifies
\(Y \sim YQ\) for \(Q \in O(k)\), and reports quotient dimension
\(kn - k(k-1)/2\). The §2-probe review (=plan-section-2-probe.org=
Probes A and B) showed that the previous plan's "embedded vs
quotient" framing conflated two orthogonal questions: /the metric/
(both options use the same horizontal projection) and /the storage
shape/ for tangent vectors (ambient \((n,k)\) matrices vs.
horizontal-basis coordinates of length \(kn - k(k-1)/2\)).

*Disposition:* v2.0 ships /ambient-shape storage/ for tangent vectors
on =PSDFixedRank=. A tangent vector =T= at point =Y= is represented
as an array of shape =(n, k)= (the same shape as =Y=). The horizontal
projection is performed by =manifold.projection= whenever it is
needed; the projection's image, expressed in ambient-shape
coordinates, has rank =manifold.dimension - manifold.gauge_dim= but
is stored in an ambient =n*k= -shaped buffer.

*Reasoning (overriding horizontal-basis storage):*

1. /Pymanopt projection convention./ Pymanopt's =projection=
   (=psd.py:30-34=) is the horizontal projection: input and output
   are both =(n,k)= ambient matrices. Ambient-shape storage means
   every pymanopt parity test compares array-to-array with no basis
   transformation; horizontal-basis storage would require us to
   maintain a basis-change step inside every parity assertion.
2. /JIT/vmap-friendliness./ Tangent vectors in ambient storage have
   a fixed shape, independent of the point. JAX's =jit= and =vmap=
   compile cleanly; horizontal-basis representation would require a
   per-point basis matrix carried alongside, breaking
   shape-statically-known compilation.
3. /Gauge bookkeeping is cheap./ The gauge nullspace dimension
   =gauge_dim = k*(k-1)//2= is exposed as a class attribute on
   =PSDFixedRank= and surfaced to the user as
   =result.diagnostics.gauge_nullspace_dim=. Users who need
   horizontal-basis coordinates can apply the basis change in
   post-processing (deferred to v2.1).

This decision propagates to Phase 2 (operator definitions return
=(n,k)= arrays), Phase 5 (info matrix sized
=(manifold.dimension, manifold.dimension)= where
=manifold.dimension = n*k=, the /ambient/ dimension; rank is
=n*k - k(k-1)/2 = dimension - gauge_dim=, *not*
=dimension - 2*gauge_dim=), and Phase 6 (labels and
=Sigma_theta_basis= reflect ambient indexing).

*Definition (load-bearing):* =manifold.dimension= is the /ambient/
\(n k\) for =PSDFixedRank(n, k)=. =manifold.gauge_dim= is \(k(k-1)/2\).
The /information dimension/ \(\dim_{\mathrm{info}} =
\mathrm{dimension} - \mathrm{gauge\_dim}\) is the rank of the
asymptotic information matrix and the rank of =Sigma_theta_arr=.
The Phase 5 rank assertion is
=rank(Sigma_theta_arr) == manifold.dimension - manifold.gauge_dim=
(equivalently, =rank == n*k - k(k-1)/2= for =PSDFixedRank(n,k)=).

This replaces the previous plan's =manifold.dimension = n*k -
k(k-1)/2= (the quotient dimension) and reconciles Phase 2/Phase 5
arithmetic that previously double-counted the gauge subtraction.

** §2.2 Drop the "embedded vs quotient" framing --- DECIDED: recast as ambient vs horizontal-basis

The =plan-section-2-probe.org= review (Probe A and Verdict §2.1)
flagged that "embedded metric" terminology misleads a pymanopt-savvy
reader: pymanopt's projection is /already/ the horizontal projection,
so both options were quotient-aware. The previous plan's framing
implied the embedded route skipped the Lyapunov solve; in fact both
routes pay that cost.

*Disposition:* throughout this revision, the language is
/ambient-shape storage with gauge-nullspace bookkeeping/ (v2.0) vs.
/horizontal-basis-coordinate storage/ (deferred to v2.1). The word
"embedded" is reserved for the metric (Boumal §3.6: the metric
inherited from the ambient inner product), which is unambiguously
what we use under both candidate storage choices. The word
"quotient" is reserved for the manifold structure itself, which is
what =PSDFixedRank= /is/, not a runtime option.

** §2.3 Tangent-space inner product as a per-manifold method --- DECIDED: =manifold.inner_product= (unchanged from v1 plan)

The literature sweep (=plan-section-13-literature.org= §2.2 verdict)
and the §2-probe (§2.2 verdict) both endorse the previous plan: expose
=manifold.inner_product(point, u, v)= as a public method (Boumal
convention), with a JAX-PyTree default implementation for subclasses
that don't override.

*Disposition:* unchanged from the previous plan. Recorded here because
it is referenced by Phase 1 protocol surface and by the Phase 4
=RiemannianLM= tangent-space least-squares solver.

** §2.4 =Sigma_theta= exposure --- DECIDED: tangent-space coordinates with basis recorded

The previous plan defaulted to tangent-space coordinates at
\(\hat\theta\) with an =Sigma_theta_ambient()= accessor for lifted
form and deferred the pull-back to v3+. The literature sweep
(=plan-section-13-literature.org= Q3 verdict) vindicates this default
(it is the only one the intrinsic Cramér--Rao literature endorses)
and adds two requirements: an explicit =Sigma_theta_basis= record on
=EstimationResult= (Smith 2005 §III.B) and the spectral-gap
diagnostic in =Diagnostics= (Boumal--Singer 2014 §6.2).

The §2-probe (Probe A under §2.3) raised a labelling concern: with
ambient-shape storage, the labels =L_t_00..L_t_{n-1,k-1}= number
\(n k\) entries but only \(n k - k(k-1)/2\) of them carry independent
information. The fix lives in Phase 6: the labels reflect the
ambient indexing, and =Sigma_theta_basis= identifies which directions
are gauge.

*Disposition:* default to tangent-space coordinates (option 1 from
the previous plan §2.3); add =result.Sigma_theta_basis= as a
first-class field; add =result.Sigma_theta_ambient()= accessor;
add a spectral-gap diagnostic; defer the pull-back along
\(Y \mapsto YY^\top\) to v2.1 (tracked as a separate issue).

** §2.5 v2.0 minimum acceptance --- DECIDED: full =PSDFixedRank=

The §2-probe Probe B for §2.4 confirmed the previous plan's call:
"v2.0 = Euclidean only" is the lower-risk path, but K-Aggregators is
blocked otherwise. =MAX_K=3= in K-Aggregators puts the gauge fraction
at 25% of the quotient dimension, well-separated from numerical
noise.

*Disposition:* ship the full thing in v2.0. Flag the gauge-fraction
scaling in Phase 2's docstring: at \(K \to m\) the gauge dominates
and the spectral-gap diagnostic becomes unreliable. Users in that
regime should switch to horizontal-basis storage (v2.1).

** §2.6 Optimiser protocol surface --- DECIDED: dual protocols with dispatch on parameter type

The plan-fidelity review's first HIGH finding (§"Optimizer protocol
surface conflict between Phase 4 and Phase 5") flagged that the
previous plan introduced =RiemannianOptimizer= without saying how it
coexists with v1's flat-vector =Optimizer= or how =estimate=
dispatches.

*Disposition:* v2 keeps the v1 =Optimizer= protocol unchanged (it
takes =Float[Array, " K"]= for =theta_init= and a flat-vector
=residual_fn=, satisfied by =_OptimistixLM= at =optimizer.py:60-117=
and =_ScipyLM= at =optimizer.py:189-193=). v2 /adds/ a
=RiemannianOptimizer= protocol that takes
=(residual_fn: Callable[[ParamsPyTree], Array], theta_init:
ParamsPyTree, manifold_spec)=. =estimate= dispatches on the
=manifold_spec= produced by Phase 1's =flatten_params=:

- /All leaves Euclidean/. =estimate= uses the v1 =Optimizer=
  protocol (no change for v1 users). The user's =optimistix_lm()=
  call site is unchanged. Internally, =estimate= recognises the v1
  protocol from the user-supplied optimiser's signature and routes
  to v1 semantics. =OptimistixManifoldAdapter= is /not/ involved
  on this path; it exists only for the case where the user
  explicitly requests a Riemannian context with an all-Euclidean
  tree.
- /Any leaf has a non-trivial ManifoldParam/. =estimate= requires
  a =RiemannianOptimizer=. The default if the user passes nothing
  is =RiemannianLM()=. If the user passes the v1 =optimistix_lm()=
  while having a non-Euclidean leaf, =estimate= raises a clear
  =TypeError= directing them to =RiemannianLM= or
  =OptimistixManifoldAdapter(optimistix_lm())=.
- /=OptimistixManifoldAdapter= for the all-Euclidean case./ This
  adapter wraps a v1 =Optimizer= (e.g., =optimistix_lm()=) and
  presents the =RiemannianOptimizer= signature so it can be passed
  as =optimizer=...= to =estimate= when the caller wants the
  Riemannian dispatch path. For default v1 usage on an
  all-Euclidean tree the adapter is not required.

The dispatch is a separate task in Phase 5
(=_resolve_optimizer(manifold_spec, user_optimizer)=). The
user-visible API is unchanged for v1 users.

** §2.7 =manifold_spec= definition --- DECIDED: frozen dataclass with =jdc.static_field= semantics

The plan-fidelity review's second HIGH finding (§"=manifold_spec= is
referenced but never defined") demanded an explicit type.

*Disposition:* =manifold_spec= is a frozen
=@dataclasses.dataclass(frozen=True)= (hashable) named =ManifoldSpec=
with the following fields, all consumed by downstream phases:

#+BEGIN_SRC python
@dataclass(frozen=True)
class ManifoldSpec:
    leaf_specs: tuple[LeafSpec, ...]    # per-leaf metadata in PyTree-leaf-walk order
    total_ambient_dim: int              # sum of ambient sizes per leaf
    total_dimension: int                # sum of manifold.dimension across leaves
    total_gauge_dim: int                # sum of manifold.gauge_dim across leaves

@dataclass(frozen=True)
class LeafSpec:
    offset: int                         # offset into the flat ambient buffer
    ambient_shape: tuple[int, ...]
    manifold: ManifoldParam             # the manifold instance
    field_name: str | None              # the user dataclass field name (None for unnamed leaves)
#+END_SRC

=ManifoldSpec= is jit-hashable (because =@dataclass(frozen=True)=
generates =__hash__=) and can be used as a =jdc.static_field= on
=ManifoldLeaf= or passed as a static argument to =jit=. The
=manifold= inside =LeafSpec= is itself a frozen JDC dataclass whose
concrete fields are sizes (no traced arrays), so the hash composes
correctly.

The =ManifoldParam= protocol surface specified in Phase 1 is what
=LeafSpec.manifold= satisfies:

- =dimension: int=
- =gauge_dim: int= (defaults to 0 for non-quotient manifolds; see §2.9
  below; required by protocol)
- =ambient_shape: tuple[int, ...]=
- =projection(point, vector) -> tangent_vector=
- =retraction(point, tangent_vector) -> point=
- =inner_product(point, u, v) -> scalar=
- =norm(point, tangent_vector) -> scalar=
- =euclidean_to_riemannian_gradient(point, euclidean_gradient) -> tangent=
- =vector_transport(point_a, point_b, tangent_vector_a) -> tangent=
- =distance(point_a, point_b) -> scalar= (optional; defaults provided)
- =random_point(key) -> point=
- =random_tangent_vector(key, point) -> tangent=
- =zero_vector(point) -> tangent=

*Why both =treedef= and =manifold_spec= (not redundant)?* The
v1 =treedef= alone cannot carry per-leaf =ambient_shape= because the
v1 invariant uses 0-d leaves uniformly. =manifold_spec= carries the
per-leaf metadata; =treedef= carries the structural skeleton. Both
are required for unflatten.

** §2.8 =ManifoldLeaf= back-compat for v1 trees --- DECIDED: scalar leaves stay scalar

The plan-fidelity review's HIGH finding on §3 (=ManifoldLeaf= breaks
the documented 0-d-leaf invariant at =params.py:52-56=) demanded a
concrete answer for existing v1 dataclasses (=EulerParams(beta=0.95,
gamma=2.0)= and the like) that don't wrap leaves in =ManifoldLeaf=.

*Disposition:* =flatten_params= on a v1-style tree (every leaf is a
0-d scalar, no =ManifoldLeaf= wrapping) produces a flat array
/bitwise identical/ to v1. The =manifold_spec= for such a tree has
=total_ambient_dim = total_dimension = K_v1=, =total_gauge_dim = 0=,
and one =LeafSpec= per scalar with =ambient_shape=()=, =manifold=
=Euclidean()= (a /zero-dimensional shape/ Euclidean with
=ambient_shape=()= and =dimension=1=).

*Crucially:* the implicit =Euclidean(1)= wrapping referenced in the
previous plan is dropped. A 0-d scalar v1 leaf maps to =Euclidean()=
(empty shape, =dimension=1=), /not/ =Euclidean(1)= (shape =(1,)=,
=dimension=1=). The two have the same flat size but the
=ambient_shape= distinction matters for the round-trip: =Euclidean()=
unflattens to a 0-d JAX scalar, =Euclidean(1)= would unflatten to a
1-D length-1 array, breaking v1 dataclass field types.

The bitwise-identical contract is verified in Phase 1 by running all
v1 acceptance tests against the v2 flatten/unflatten path: any v1
tree must produce the same flat array, the same =treedef= (up to the
v2 augmentation), and the same reconstruction.

** §2.9 CLAUDE.md commitment 4 reconciliation: CU + Riemannian Jacobian --- DECIDED: derive in Phase 5

The plan-fidelity review's HIGH finding on commitment 4 demanded that
the plan reconcile "Don't silently drop the \(\nabla_\theta V\) term"
(CLAUDE.md commitment 4) with Phase 5's use of =measure.jacobian=
on moments (rather than on residuals).

*Disposition:* under CU on a Riemannian manifold, the asymptotic
covariance is \((G^\top V^{-1} G)^{-1}\) with =G= the /Riemannian/
Jacobian of moments, by the same Z-estimator argument as in
van der Vaart (1998) §5.4. The \(\nabla_\theta V\) term enters the CU
/gradient/ during optimisation but vanishes in the asymptotic info
matrix because the FOC sets the relevant gradient component to zero.

Phase 5 must:

1. Build the residual function so that JAX AD picks up the
   \(\nabla_\theta V\) dependence through CU during optimisation
   (preserves CLAUDE.md commitment 4 for the optimiser; matches v1
   pattern at =estimator.py:184-190=).
2. Use =measure.jacobian= directly on moments (not on residuals) for
   the post-hoc info matrix at \(\hat\theta\) (matches v1
   =estimator.py:202-214=).
3. Apply =manifold.euclidean_to_riemannian_gradient= per leaf so
   =G_hat= is the /Riemannian/ Jacobian of moments at \(\hat\theta\);
   the info matrix is =G_riem.T @ Lambda @ G_riem=.

A written derivation lives in the Phase 8 companion note
=docs/mcar-asymptotics-riemannian.org= (subsection "CU + Riemannian:
the \(\nabla_\theta V\) term") citing van der Vaart §5.4. A Phase 5
test exercises a CU residual under a =ManifoldParam= and verifies
the optimiser converges (commitment 4 honoured) and the Phase 5
info matrix agrees with a numerical-derivative reference within
=rtol=1e-4=.

The same reconciliation applies to v1 today (=estimator.py:202=); v2
does not change this contract.

** §2.10 =tangent_basis_names= moved to Phase 1 --- DECIDED

The plan-fidelity Phase-dependency finding (§"Phase 6 depends on

*Disposition:* =tangent_basis_names(manifold_spec, param_names)= is
specified in Phase 1 alongside the =ManifoldParam= protocol. It is a
pure function from =(ManifoldSpec, tuple[str, ...])= to
=tuple[str, ...]= and Phase 5 uses it directly to label
=Sigma_theta=. Phase 6 retains the =components()= accessor and
=Sigma_theta_basis= recording, but the label generation is no longer
a Phase 6 surprise.

** §2.11 =J_dof= contract change --- DECIDED: explicit Phase 5 task

The plan-fidelity review's MEDIUM finding (=J_dof= documented in §13
but no Phase 5 task) and the §13 literature sweep's Q4 finding both
demanded explicit handling. The literature sweep also added a
contract requirement: the residual function must be gauge-invariant
for =J_dof = M - dim_info= to be correct.

*Disposition:* Phase 5 has an explicit task to change
=J_dof = M - K_v1= (v1, =estimator.py:219=) to
=J_dof = M - (manifold.dimension - manifold.gauge_dim) = M -
dim_info= (v2). The contract --- /the residual function must be
gauge-invariant in the sense that \(\nabla_\theta g\) lies in the
horizontal subspace at \(\theta_0\)/ --- is added to Phase 5
acceptance and a deliberately-violating test asserts that =J_stat=
fails calibration when it is broken.

For non-quotient manifolds (e.g., =Euclidean=, full-rank SPD) where
=gauge_dim = 0=, the rule reduces to
=J_dof = M - manifold.dimension=, which collapses to the v1 form
when =manifold.dimension == K_v1=.

** §2.12 Phase 4 acceptance criterion --- DECIDED: split into two criteria

The plan-fidelity review's HIGH finding (§"Acceptance criterion for
Phase 4 RiemannianLM is vague") and the §2-probe Verdict §2.4
demanded a concrete, automatable test.

*Disposition:* Phase 4 acceptance splits into two criteria:

(a) /All-Euclidean equivalence with v1 =optimistix_lm=/. For an
all-Euclidean parameter PyTree, =OptimistixManifoldAdapter= +
=optimistix_lm()= produces output bitwise identical

Pseudocode for one step:

#+BEGIN_SRC text
At theta_k:
  r_k       = residual_fn(theta_k)              # (M,) ambient
  J_amb_k   = jacfwd(residual_fn)(theta_k)      # PyTree of (M, *factor_shape)

  # min_d ||J_riem_k * d - r_k||^2 + lambda * ||d||^2
  d_k       = solve_tangent_lstsq(J_riem_k, r_k, lambda_k,

  of =theta= --- one ambient-shape block per leaf. Each block
  becomes a per-factor block of the tangent-space Jacobian after

- The tangent-space Gauss--Newton solve uses the manifold's
  inner-product Gram matrix; for =Euclidean= leaves this reduces to
  the standard normal equation; for =PSDFixedRank= it remains a
  least-squares solve in ambient \(Y\)-space because the embedded
  metric (Boumal §3.6) coincides with the ambient Frobenius (per
  §2.2 framing).
- /Gauge-aware LM floor./ Per the plan-fidelity MEDIUM finding on
  the tangent-LS solve: =lambda_k= has a /floor/:

  small constant chosen to prevent the LM normal equation from
  becoming numerically singular along the gauge subspace as
  =lambda_k -> 0= on accept steps. The previous plan's
  =lambda <- 0.5 * lambda= on accept could shrink =lambda_k= to
  zero; the floor prevents the gauge directions from going
  singular.
- LM damping schedule: =lambda <- max(gauge_floor, 0.5 * lambda)=

- Convergence: norm of Riemannian gradient

* Section 13: Open Theoretical Questions

These are questions the v1 mcar-asymptotics note doesn't answer and
that Phase 5--6 implementations will surface concretely. Each entry
records the literature-sweep finding (=docs/reviews/plan-section-
13-literature.org=) and the v2 plan disposition.

** Gauge identification under quotient geometry (Q1)

For =PSDFixedRank(n, k)=, the K(K-1)/2 -dimensional gauge subgroup
(=O(k)= acting on the right of \(Y\)) means \(\theta_0\) is only
identified up to an equivalence class. The literature sweep
(Boumal--Singer 2014 §3.2 + Lemma 3.4; Boumal 2013) confirms:

1. /J-test asymptotics/: \(J \sim \chi^2_{M - \dim_{\mathrm{info}}}\)
   where \(\dim_{\mathrm{info}} = \mathrm{dimension} -
   \mathrm{gauge\_dim}\). Phase 5 implements the change explicitly
   (§2.11).
2. /=Sigma_theta= well-defined/: yes on the tangent space (Phase 5
   constructs it as the count-based pinv).

Open sub-question: does pairwise overlap (the v1 MCAR condition)
suffice for positive-definiteness of the /horizontal-block/ info
matrix? Tracked as a separate v2.1 issue (MCAR-on-quotient PD
companion note).

** Sigma_theta in non-Euclidean tangent spaces (Q3 in lit sweep)

The Cramér--Rao bound generalises to manifolds (Smith 2005 §III.B;
Boumal 2023 §3.6). =Sigma_theta_arr= reports dispersion of
\(\hat\theta\) along geodesics emanating from \(\theta_0\). For
=Euclidean=, geodesics are straight lines; for =PSDFixedRank=,
geodesics are exponentials approximated by retraction. The
\(O(\|v\|^2)\) error of retraction-vs-exp is asymptotically
negligible. Phase 6 records the basis (=Sigma_theta_basis=) and
provides =wald_ellipsoid(level)=. README quickstart flags the
geodesic interpretation.

** Consistency and asymptotic normality on the manifold (Q2)

Per literature sweep: Smith 2005 Thm 4 (Lie group with bi-invariant
metric) + Thm 5 (general Riemannian) + Newey--McFadden 1994 §3 (GMM
regularity translated via Boumal §5.3). Mechanical translation; no
new theorem required. Phase 8 companion note records the proof.

** Bootstrap and vmap

Per literature sweep §Q3 and the §2-probe Probe B under §2.3. The
=RiemannianLM= solver is JIT/vmap-friendly when every factor is
native. =vmap(estimate)(bootstrap_keys)= produces replicates;
empirical-covariance comparison to =Sigma_theta_arr= requires
manifold alignment via =log=. Documented in Phase 6 docstring.

** CU + Riemannian \(\nabla_\theta V\)

Per §2.9 disposition. CU FOC argument (van der Vaart §5.4) gives
\((G^\top V^{-1} G)^{-1}\) as the correct asymptotic info matrix
with =G= the Riemannian Jacobian of moments. The residual closure
preserves the \(\nabla_\theta V\) dependence for the optimiser
(CLAUDE.md commitment 4). Derivation in companion note.
