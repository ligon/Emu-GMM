* Section 1: Scope and Sequencing

This plan operationalises *Path A* of the pymanopt-integration memo
([[file:design-notes/pymanopt-integration.org][docs/design-notes/pymanopt-integration.org]], PR #27) as a sequence of
phases that extend v1 ([[file:implementation-plan.org][docs/implementation-plan.org]], Phases 1--8 DONE).
The architectural mandate from the memo: JAX-native implementations of
=Euclidean=, =PSDFixedRank=, =Product= plus a Riemannian Gauss--Newton
NLLS solver, with an optional =from_pymanopt(M)= adapter for exotic
manifolds. K-Aggregators's =Product(PSDFixedRank(m, K), Euclidean(...),
Euclidean(...))= is the concrete v2 acceptance target.

Sequencing:

1. /Manifold foundations/ --- the type machinery (=ManifoldParam=
   protocol, relaxed PyTree contract) plus the trivial =Euclidean=
   reference. Reproduces v1 behaviour on the existing acceptance
   tests; no inference changes yet.
2. /PSDFixedRank/ --- the hard one. Numerical-equivalence tests
   against pymanopt's NumPy implementation.
3. /Product manifold/ --- composition; per-factor dispatch.
4. /RiemannianLM/ --- NLLS-on-a-manifold solver with retraction at
   each step. =OptimistixManifoldAdapter= so the v1 API still works
   when every leaf is Euclidean.
5. /Estimator + Jacobian/ --- manifold-aware unflatten; Riemannian
   info matrix; gauge-aware condition number.
6. /Result + Diagnostics/ --- =theta_hat= carries manifold structure;
   =.components()= accessor (K-Agg idiom); tangent-space
   =Sigma_theta=; =gauge_nullspace_dim=.
7. /Optional pymanopt adapter/ --- =from_pymanopt(M)= under the
   =[manifold]= extra; advertised non-JIT.
8. /Polish/ --- v2 quickstart, CLAUDE.md update, deferrals refresh.

Each phase has dependencies, tasks, and an acceptance criterion. The
first non-trivial end-to-end run is at the end of Phase 5
(=PSDFixedRank= recovery via =RiemannianLM=). The full K-Aggregators
target shape (=Product(PSDFixedRank, Euclidean, Euclidean)=) lights up
at the end of Phase 6.

Effort estimates follow the v1 convention: /S/ ≈ 1--2 hours, /M/ ≈
half-day, /L/ ≈ full day, /XL/ ≈ multi-day. Calibrated against
/Sue + Claude pair-of-hours/, not solo developer time.

* Section 2: Pre-Implementation Decisions

A small number of choices need to land before any v2 code is written.
Each materially affects how downstream phases look.

** TODO Decide embedded vs quotient metric on =PSDFixedRank=          :M:

Pymanopt's =PSDFixedRank= (=psd.py:7-70=) parameterises
\(X = Y Y^\top\) with the canonical Euclidean metric on \(Y\) and
identifies \(Y \sim YQ\) for orthogonal \(Q \in O(k)\); the quotient
geometry has dimension \(kn - k(k-1)/2\). The K-Aggregators code
needs =exclude_gauge=True= semantics (gauge: the K(K-1)/2 nullspace
on a rank-K factor).

Two options:

1. /Embedded metric/ (= the ambient Euclidean inner product on \(Y\)).
   Easier: =projection=, =retraction=, =inner_product= all match the
   pymanopt source exactly. Gauge direction shows up in
   =Sigma_theta= as a K(K-1)/2 -dimensional nullspace that the user
   must filter out.
2. /Quotient metric/ (= the projection to the horizontal subspace at
   each step). The same as pymanopt's projection step (see
   =psd.py:30-34=, the Lyapunov solve), but consistently applied
   inside =RiemannianLM=. =Sigma_theta= then lives on the quotient
   tangent space and the gauge is intrinsically zero rather than
   marginalised out.

/Recommendation pending decision:/ embedded metric, with an explicit
=gauge_nullspace_dim= field on =EstimationResult= (Phase 6) and a
=Sigma_theta= condition-number check that treats the K(K-1)/2 small
eigenvalues as expected gauge. This matches pymanopt's existing
geometry and Boumal §7. The quotient route is cleaner but adds a
Lyapunov solve at every step of =RiemannianLM=, and the K-Aggregators
gauge filter is an established pattern.

This decision lives at the top because it propagates: Phase 2's
operator definitions, Phase 5's info-matrix shape, and Phase 6's
=Sigma_theta= interpretation all depend on it.

** TODO Tangent-space inner product convention                        :S:

The tangent space at \(Y \in \mathrm{PSDFixedRank}\) inherits the
Frobenius inner product \(\langle \dot Y_1, \dot Y_2 \rangle =
\mathrm{tr}(\dot Y_1^\top \dot Y_2)\); pymanopt does this via
=np.tensordot(..., axes=tangent_vector.ndim)= (=psd.py:17-22=). For
=Product=, the inner product is the sum across factors
(=product.py:64-67=).

Decision needed: do we expose =manifold.inner_product(point, u, v)= as
a public method (Boumal convention) or fold it into the more
JAX-natural pattern of "tangent vectors are JAX PyTrees and the inner
product is =jax.tree_util.tree_reduce(jnp.sum, jax.tree_map(jnp.vdot,
u, v))="? The latter composes with =vmap= and =jit= more cleanly but
hides the Riemannian structure from users porting from pymanopt.

/Recommendation pending decision:/ expose =manifold.inner_product= as
the canonical surface and provide a JAX-PyTree default implementation
when a subclass doesn't override.

** TODO Decide how =Sigma_theta= is exposed                           :M:

Three positions are defensible:

1. /Tangent-space coordinates at \(\hat\theta\)./ Pure: the asymptotic
   covariance lives where the central limit theorem on the manifold
   says it should. Cost: users need a basis to interpret it. For
   =Euclidean= leaves the basis is the identity, so v1 users see no
   change; for =PSDFixedRank= the basis is the horizontal subspace.
2. /Lifted to the embedded ambient space./ For =PSDFixedRank(n, k)=,
   that is the \(n \times k\) ambient matrix space. Easy to read,
   but the K(K-1)/2 gauge directions show up as a near-zero subspace
   (regardless of the metric choice in §2.1).
3. /Lifted to the parameter-of-interest \(X = Y Y^\top\) space./ The
   geometric pull-back of =Sigma_theta= along \(Y \mapsto Y Y^\top\).
   Most useful for downstream inference on the PSD matrix itself
   (which is what K-Aggregators consumes), but most expensive to
   compute and the chain rule is non-trivial.

/Recommendation pending decision:/ default to option (1)
(tangent-space coordinates), with a =result.Sigma_theta_ambient()=
accessor for option (2). Defer option (3) to v2.1 (it requires the
pull-back machinery for any user-defined map).

This decision propagates to Phase 6 (=EstimationResult= shape).

** TODO Decide v2.0 minimum acceptance criterion                      :S:

The memo §"Suggested scoping" sketches /v2.0/ as
=PSDFixedRank + Euclidean + Product + RiemannianLM + Sigma_theta on
the tangent space=. The memo's open question #4 floats a "v2.0 ships

/Recommendation pending decision:/ ship the full thing in v2.0

** TODO src/emu_gmm/manifolds/psd_fixed_rank.py                        :L:

JAX implementations of each operator. Reference =psd.py:7-70=. The
heart of the port is the Lyapunov solve in =projection=
(=psd.py:30-34=); JAX has =jax.scipy.linalg.solve= but no direct
=solve_continuous_lyapunov=. Decision: implement Bartels--Stewart
via QR/Schur on small matrices (\(k \times k\), so cheap), or use a
=jax.scipy.linalg.lu_solve= against the Kronecker form
\((I_k \otimes Y^\top Y + Y^\top Y \otimes I_k) \mathrm{vec}(\Omega)
= \mathrm{vec}(\text{AS})\). The Kronecker form is the most
straightforward to JAX-vmap; the cost is \(O(k^6)\) rather than
\(O(k^3)\), but for K-Aggregators's K=5..20 this is negligible.

- =projection(point, vector)= --- =psd.py:30-34=.
  Compute \(\Omega\) by the Lyapunov solve. Return
  =vector - point @ Omega=.
- =retraction(point, tangent_vector)= --- =psd.py:46-49=.
  Just =point + tangent_vector=; the manifold is closed under
  addition as long as rank doesn't drop (assertion documented but
  not enforced in JAX-pure path; the optimiser is responsible for
  step-size control).
- =euclidean_to_riemannian_gradient(point, euclidean_gradient)= ---
  =psd.py:38-40=. Identity (because the quotient projection happens
  on the tangent vector, not the gradient).
- =inner_product(point, u, v)= --- =psd.py:17-22=. Frobenius.
- =norm(point, tangent_vector)= --- =psd.py:27-28=. Frobenius norm.
- =distance(point_a, point_b)= --- =psd.py:24-25=. Calls =log= and
  =norm=; log per =psd.py:51-53= via SVD of \(B^\top A\).
- =vector_transport(point_a, point_b, tangent_vector_a)= --- "project
  at new point" (=psd.py:63-64=).
- =random_point(key)= --- =psd.py:55-56=. =jax.random.normal(key,
  (n, k))=.
- =random_tangent_vector(key, point)= --- =psd.py:58-61=.
  Project a random ambient vector to the tangent space and
  normalise.
- =zero_vector(point)= --- zeros of shape =(n, k)=.
- Class attribute =dimension = k * n - k * (k - 1) // 2= per
  =psd.py:113=.
- Class attribute =gauge_dim = k * (k - 1) // 2= --- new; consumed
  by Phase 6 diagnostics.

** TODO src/emu_gmm/manifolds/optimizer.py: RiemannianOptimizer       :M:

A new protocol parallel to =types.Optimizer= (=types.py:118-130=)

#+BEGIN_SRC python
class RiemannianOptimizer(Protocol):
    def __call__(
        self,
        residual_fn: Callable[[ParamsPyTree], Array],
        theta_init: ParamsPyTree,
        manifold: ManifoldParam,
    ) -> tuple[ParamsPyTree, OptimizerInfo]: ...

Note the contrast with v1: =theta_init= is the original PyTree (not

** TODO src/emu_gmm/manifolds/riemannian_lm.py                         :L:

Riemannian Gauss--Newton via per-step retraction. Boumal §6.4
("Riemannian Gauss--Newton for nonlinear least squares").

Pseudocode for one step:

#+BEGIN_SRC text
At theta_k:
  r_k       = residual_fn(theta_k)              # (M,) ambient
  J_amb_k   = jacfwd(residual_fn)(theta_k)      # PyTree of (M, *factor_shape)
  J_riem_k  = manifold.euclidean_to_riemannian_gradient(theta_k,
                                                       J_amb_k)  # per-factor
  # Solve the Gauss-Newton normal equation on the tangent space:
  # min_d ||J_riem_k * d - r_k||^2 + lambda * ||d||^2
  d_k       = solve_tangent_lstsq(J_riem_k, r_k, lambda_k,
                                  manifold.inner_product)
  # Retract:
  theta_kp1 = manifold.retraction(theta_k, d_k)
  # LM-style trust-region adjustment of lambda_k via cost ratio.
#+END_SRC

Key design decisions:

- =jacfwd= returns a PyTree of Jacobians that matches the structure
  of =theta= --- one ambient-shape block per leaf. Each block
  becomes a per-factor block of the tangent-space Jacobian after
  =euclidean_to_riemannian_gradient=.
- The tangent-space Gauss--Newton solve uses the manifold's
  inner-product Gram matrix; for =Euclidean= leaves this reduces to
  the standard normal equation; for =PSDFixedRank= it remains a
  least-squares solve in ambient \(Y\)-space because the embedded
  metric coincides with the ambient Frobenius (per §2.1
  decision).
- LM damping parameter follows Levenberg--Marquardt:
  =lambda <- 0.5 * lambda= on accept, =lambda <- 2 * lambda= on
  reject; initialise to =1e-3 * ||J^T J||_F=.
- Convergence: norm of Riemannian gradient
  =manifold.norm(theta_k, J_riem_k.T @ r_k)= below =atol + rtol *
  norm(r_k)=; max-steps cap matches v1's =max_steps=200=.
- Return =OptimizerInfo(backend="riemannian_lm", ...)= per v1's
  convention.

/Tests:/ Boumal §6.4 dictionary-recovery problem on
=PSDFixedRank(n=5, k=3)=; verify convergence to a known
\(\hat\theta\) in <50 steps with =J_stat= below 1e-10.

** TODO src/emu_gmm/manifolds/optimistix_adapter.py                    :M:

(=optimizer.py:120-143=) so it conforms to =RiemannianOptimizer=.

* Section 13: Open Theoretical Questions

These are questions the v1 mcar-asymptotics note doesn't answer and
that Phase 5--6 implementations will surface concretely.

** Gauge identification under quotient geometry

For =PSDFixedRank(n, k)=, the K(K-1)/2 -dimensional gauge subgroup
(=O(k)= acting on the right of \(Y\)) means \(\theta_0\) is only
identified up to an equivalence class. Two practical issues:

1. /Are J-test asymptotics affected?/ Hansen (1982) gives \(J \sim
   \chi^2_{M - K}\). On a manifold, the right degrees-of-freedom
   count is \(M - \dim(\mathcal{M})\), where
   \(\dim(\mathcal{M}) = kn - k(k-1)/2\). The =J_dof= in
   =estimator.py:219= becomes =M - manifold.dimension=, /not/
   =M - K_ambient=. Phase 5 implementation note.
2. /Is Sigma_theta well-defined?/ Yes on the tangent space (it is
   constructed as the inverse of a horizontal-subspace operator);
   ambiguous if reported on the ambient space (which is why §2.3
   defaults to tangent coordinates).

** Sigma_theta in non-Euclidean tangent spaces

The Cramér--Rao bound generalises to manifolds (Smith 2005; Boumal
§7.4): the asymptotic distribution at \(\hat\theta\) is
\(\sqrt{n}(\hat\theta - \theta_0) \to N_T(0, \Sigma_\theta)\) where
\(N_T\) is a tangent-space Gaussian. /What does that mean
geometrically?/ The =Sigma_theta= tells the user the dispersion of
\(\hat\theta\) /along the geodesics emanating from \(\theta_0\)/.
For =Euclidean=, geodesics are straight lines and this collapses to
the v1 picture; for =PSDFixedRank=, geodesics are exponentials of
tangent vectors (which the embedded metric approximates by
retraction). This is internally consistent but the README v2
quickstart needs to flag it for users porting from frequentist
intuition.

** Consistency and asymptotic normality on the manifold

The v1 mcar-asymptotics note ([[file:mcar-asymptotics.org][docs/mcar-asymptotics.org]]) establishes
consistency, asymptotic normality, and positive-definiteness of the
info matrix under MCAR for the Euclidean case. The Riemannian
generalisation should be straightforward: replace gradients with
Riemannian gradients, replace \(\sqrt{n}(\hat\theta - \theta_0)\)
with \(\sqrt{n} \mathrm{Log}_{\theta_0}(\hat\theta)\), and rerun
the proofs against Smith (2005). Phase 8 candidate: a short
=docs/mcar-asymptotics-riemannian.org= companion note.

** Bootstrap and vmap

The v1 commitment "estimate() must be JIT/vmap-compatible" means
bootstrap-via-vmap should work for the Riemannian case too. The
=RiemannianLM= solver is pure JAX and =jit=/=vmap=-friendly by
construction; the open question is whether =Sigma_theta_arr =
pinv(info_matrix)= (Phase 5) survives =vmap=. =jnp.linalg.pinv=
does support =vmap=, but the gauge-dimension rcond threshold may
need to be a static field rather than data-dependent. Worth a
spot-check at the end of Phase 5.

* Section 14: Out of Scope for This Plan

Things this plan does /not/ schedule:

- /Stiefel/, /Grassmann/, /Sphere/, /Hyperbolic/ as JAX-native
  manifolds. Available via =from_pymanopt= (Phase 7) but not in the
  core; v3+ candidates.
- /Riemannian trust-region/ (Boumal §6.5). v2 uses
  Gauss--Newton-with-retraction; TR can be added without breaking
  the =RiemannianOptimizer= protocol.
- /Quotient-metric =PSDFixedRank=/. v2.0 ships the embedded metric
  per the §2.1 decision; quotient is a v2.1 candidate if gauge
  marginalisation proves awkward in practice.
- /=Sigma_theta= pull-back to a parameter-of-interest map/
  (§2.3 option 3). Requires a general framework for pull-back of
  covariance along a user-specified map; v3+.
- /Geodesic-rather-than-retraction inner loop/. The retraction is a
  first-order approximation to =exp=; for problems where the
  second-order error matters, =exp= is a future improvement.
