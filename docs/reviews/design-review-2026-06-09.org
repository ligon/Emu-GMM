#+TITLE: Critical Review: emu-gmm Design Document
#+AUTHOR: Sue (for Ethan Ligon)
#+DATE: 2026-06-09

Read =docs/design.org= twice and verified the parts that depend on the code
rather than the prose. Honest critical read, biggest issues first. Substantive
econometrics is separated from architecture/framing from smaller points, with a
calibrating note on what is genuinely strong at the end.

* Substantive (econometric) issues

** 1. The marquee application likely violates the one assumption the missing-data machinery rests on
The whole per-coordinate / available-pairs apparatus is honest that it only
carries the standard results under *MCAR* (\S5 Open Questions, item 1;
design.org:351). But the flagship example is the Hansen--Singleton multi-asset
Euler equation, and the motivating missingness is "asset returns for
non-holders" (design.org:430). That is the textbook /limited-participation/
problem, and it is close to the worst case for MCAR: whether household \(i\)
holds asset \(j\) is correlated with wealth/constraints, which is correlated
with the SDF, hence with \(\psi_j\) itself. So \(d_{ij}\) is correlated with
\(\psi_j\) (MNAR), and the per-coordinate mean
\(\frac{1}{N_j}\sum_i d_{ij} w_i \psi_j\) converges to
\(\mathbb{E}[\psi_j \mid \text{holds } j]\), not \(\mathbb{E}[\psi_j]\). For a
non-holder the asset-\(j\) FOC is an /inequality/, so the equality moment does
not even hold conditionally.

This is not a code bug; it is that the convenience feature (drop per coordinate,
not per row) *silently changes the estimand exactly in the use case the doc
leads with*. The MCAR caveat is doing very heavy lifting and is buried in "Open
Theoretical Questions." Promote it to the front and either (a) pick a flagship
example where MCAR is defensible, or (b) say plainly that the Euler/non-holder
case needs a participation correction the framework does not provide.

** 2. The regularized regime is the default under any real missingness, not an edge case
Available-pairs \(V_X\) is not PSD in finite samples (design.org:127), so
=DiagonalTikhonov= fires, and a binding \(\tau\) turns the \(J\) limit into a
generalized \(\chi^2\) handled by Welch--Satterthwaite (design.org:339-341).
That is all correct and honest. But the implication is under-stated: in the
target data (CEX with non-holders, hence genuine missingness), the clean
\(\chi^2_{M-K}\) is the /exception/ and the moment-matched approximation is the
/rule/. The headline inferential output is, generically, the approximate one.
State this up front, with some sense of how often \(\tau\) actually binds on
realistic data, rather than leaving it in \S5.

** 3. Verified code-level asymmetry: the J-stat is adjusted for regularization but the standard errors are not
Checked the source. =Sigma_theta = (G'(V*)^{-1}G)^{-1}= using the /regularized/
weighting, via =info_local = Z'Z=, =Z = L^{-1}G=, =L = chol(V*)=
(estimator.py:703-711). This is inverse-information, *not a sandwich*. It equals
the true asymptotic variance only when \(\Lambda = V(\hat\theta)^{-1}\) exactly,
i.e. CU/iterated at convergence with \(\tau = 0\). It is mis-stated whenever:

- \(\tau\) binds (the generic missing-data case from point 2) :: the correct
  meat is \(G'\Lambda V \Lambda G\) with the /unregularized/ \(V\), and
  \(\Lambda V \neq I\).
- weighting is =Identity= (offered "for diagnostic runs," design.org:301) or
  =Fixed(V0)= / two-step where \(V_0 \neq V(\hat\theta_2)\).

So the same \(\tau\) that triggers the careful Welch--Satterthwaite adjustment
on \(J\) (estimator.py:725-729) gets /no/ corresponding correction on the SEs,
even though the unregularized =V_local= is right there in scope at the SE site
(estimator.py:726). And =CLAUDE.md= / =implementation-plan= call
\((G'\Lambda G)^{-1}\) "the natural-scale sandwich," which is a misnomer that
may be masking this. Every first-class analytic SE (=standard_errors=,
=functional_se=, =gamma_se=, =eigenvalue_se=, =coef_table=) inherits it. The
cluster bootstrap is the escape hatch, but nothing flags when the analytic
default is silently invalid. This is the most concrete "fix or document" item.

** 4. CU-as-default sits uneasily with the Gauss-Newton/LM solver, precisely under the alternative the J-test cares about
Framing \(Q = \lVert y(\theta)\rVert^2\), \(y = L(\theta)^{-1} m(\theta)\) and
handing it to Levenberg--Marquardt is elegant, and \S4 is right that JAX keeps
the exact /gradient/ \(2J'y\) including \(\partial L^{-1}/\partial\theta\) (so
you are not silently dropping \(\nabla_\theta V\)). But LM's /step/ uses the
Gauss-Newton Hessian \(J'J\), which omits \(2\sum_i y_i \nabla^2 y_i\). That
term vanishes only as \(y \to 0\), i.e. under correct specification. Under
overidentification with any misspecification (the regime where the J-test is
interesting), \(y(\hat\theta)\) is bounded away from 0 and the omitted curvature
is exactly the CU \(L^{-1}\) curvature \S4 emphasizes. So the solver's curvature
model is least accurate in the CU-specific term the design is proud of keeping.
It still converges to a stationary point of the exact \(Q\), but step quality
and basin behavior can degrade under interesting alternatives. Worth checking
whether CU should use a genuine trust region on \(Q\) with the full Hessian
(which \S4 essentially derives) rather than GN.

** 5. Fixed-\(\tau\) anchoring can break the CU solve mid-search
\(\tau\) is chosen once at \(\theta_\text{init}\) and held fixed "for cross-fit
comparability" and smoothness (design.org:319). But under CU, \(V_\mu(\theta)\)
varies over \(\theta\) and can become /more/ nearly-singular than at the anchor,
especially far from the optimum where CU drives \(V\) toward the moments'
degeneracies. A \(\tau\) sized for PD-ness at \(\theta_\text{init}\) gives no PD
guarantee elsewhere, so the optimizer can step into a region where
\(V^\star(\theta)\) fails Cholesky and the criterion returns NaN. You have
chosen smoothness over guaranteed-PD-everywhere; that is a legitimate choice,
but the CU failure mode (NaN mid-solve) is not grappled with, and it interacts
with points 3 and 4.

* Architecture / framing

** 6. "Variance is orthogonal to integration" is composability, not independence
True and valuable as a statement about /inputs/: you can swap
=CovarianceStrategy= without touching the =Measure=, and the
residual/point-estimate path is genuinely design-invariant (design.org:145, a
real win). But the /outputs/ under the default CU weighting (and under
two-step) depend on the covariance strategy: \(\hat\theta\), \(J\), and the SEs
are all functions of \(V\). The headline sentence reads like a claim of
statistical orthogonality. Recast it as input-separability / independent
substitutability and keep the strong claim ("only \(V_X\) changes,
\(\mathbb{E}_\mu[\psi]\) does not") where it is actually true.

** 7. The "one measure \(\mathbb{E}_\mu\)" thesis has an asterisk on its most important instance
The flagship =EmpiricalMeasure= is explicitly an \(M\)-tuple of coordinate
measures, "not a strict measure-theoretic object" (design.org:68, 88). And the
covariance is irreducibly /joint/: \([V_X]_{jk}\) needs the rows where both
\(j,k\) are observed, so the per-coordinate-measure picture is clean only on the
diagonal. The doc hedges this honestly but quietly. For your target reader
(mathematically literate economists), make the hedge loud and up front, and sell
the unifying claim as "one polymorphic /operator interface/" rather than "one
measure," so the elegant frame does not overpromise.

** 8. The doc cannot be used as a v1 contract without cross-referencing three other files
It interleaves settled v1 architecture, deferred v2/v3
(=ReplicateWeightCovariance=, SMM matching, Higham/eigen-floor regularizers,
Davies/Imhof), prospective sketches (=ParameterLaw=), and open theory, with
inline status tags but no single "what is actually in v1" boundary. Given how
much you care about separation of concerns, the document itself would benefit
from that separation: a short v1-contract surface vs roadmap vs theory-debt.
Right now a reader has to hold all 439 lines plus =CLAUDE.md= plus
=implementation-plan.org= plus =api-sketch.org= to know what is real.

* Smaller / verify-later

- Notation collision on \(\mathcal{I}\) and \(V\) :: \S1 defines \(V(\theta)\)
  as the /per-observation/ variance (design.org:23-24) and
  \(\mathcal{I} = G'\Lambda G\) as the per-observation (Cramer-Rao-analogue)
  information, but \S5 uses \(V_X\) = variance /of the mean/ (\(O(1/N)\)) and the
  same \(\mathcal{I}\) symbol becomes the full-sample precision
  \(\Sigma_\theta^{-1}\). Same letters, factor-of-\(N\) different meanings. The
  "sample-level convention" note (design.org:25) flags the \(V\) side; the
  \(\mathcal{I}\) side is not disambiguated and an implementer porting from
  Hansen/Hayashi will trip.
- Diagonal-V dispatch is a hard threshold inside the regularizer
  (design.org:321) :: the \(10^{-12}\) off-diagonal trigger means two
  nearly-diagonal \(V\)'s straddling the boundary get different equivariance
  structure and different \(V^\star\). It is chosen once per estimation so the
  /surface/ stays smooth within a solve, but the map \(V \mapsto V^\star\) jumps
  /across/ estimations. You argue it only triggers in analytical setups; a
  weakly-correlated empirical \(V\) could trip it too. Low priority, but it is a
  discontinuity in an object you otherwise work hard to keep smooth.
- The worked example sidesteps the headline synthetic capability :: the bundled
  =SyntheticMeasure= sampler is \(\theta\)-independent (=del theta=,
  design.org:194), so the differentiable-\(\theta\)-dependent-simulator path (the
  whole reason for CRN + reparameterization in \S4) is asserted but never
  exercised by the shipped example. With SMM matching deferred to v2, the "three
  measures, one model" triptych in v1 is really "empirical estimates, analytical
  diagnoses, synthetic round-trips." Honest, but the symmetry oversells current
  capability.
- \S6 both-sided label fidelity is aspirational, not realized :: it is stated as
  a design goal, then admits "the v1 implementation drops them" (Variables labels
  regenerated positionally, design.org:391). Fine to track as an issue; just do
  not present it as done.

* What is genuinely strong (so the above is calibrated)

The separation of integration from variance, with the residual path provably
design-invariant, is a real architectural win and the cleanest part of the
design. The per-pair \(H_{c,jk}\) / \(N_j N_k\) overlap discipline and the
*signed-spectrum* feasibility test for regularization (design.org:313, reject
the SVD condition number because it is sign-blind) are subtle, correct, and
clearly learned the hard way. The named-tensor dual-axis convention and the
double-=where= NaN-safety reasoning (in-domain mean sentinel, not zero, to avoid
\(0 \cdot \infty\) cotangents) are careful JAX hygiene most people get wrong.
The analytical round-trip as an /a posteriori/ identification test is a genuinely
nice idea. And the scope discipline (=ParameterLaw= explicitly gated, "file an
issue, do not reinvent the criterion") matches how this should be built.

* If I had to pick three things to act on

1. Fix or explicitly document the SE story (point 3): either sandwich when
   \(\Lambda \neq V^{-1}\) / \(\tau\) binds, or surface a flag on
   =EstimationResult= that the analytic SEs are only valid under optimal
   weighting at \(\tau = 0\) and point to the bootstrap otherwise.
2. Move the MCAR limitation and the participation/estimand problem (point 1) out
   of Open Questions and into the framing, and reconsider the flagship example.
3. Decide the CU + LM + fixed-\(\tau\) interaction (points 4-5) deliberately,
   since all three compound under the same overID-with-missingness regime.
