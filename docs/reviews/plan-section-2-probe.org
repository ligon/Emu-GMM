* Scope

Probe of the four §2 pre-implementation decisions in
=docs/implementation-plan-v2-manifold.org= (lines 51--150).  Default
posture: refuted=true.  Each section below records a probe attempted
against the recommendation, the surviving concern (if any), and a

References by file:line in this document are to
=docs/implementation-plan-v2-manifold.org= unless otherwise noted.

* §2.1 Embedded metric on =PSDFixedRank= --- SURVIVES WITH CONCERNS

** Probe A: Misnomer in "embedded vs quotient" framing

Reading pymanopt's =psd.py:30-34=, =projection= already performs the
horizontal projection via a Lyapunov solve.  Pymanopt's
=PSDFixedRank.dimension = kn - k(k-1)/2= (line 113) is the /quotient/
dimension, not the ambient =nk= dimension.  The plan's "embedded
metric" terminology (lines 70--73) is therefore misleading: the
proposal is /not/ "skip the horizontal projection".  Both options in
§2.1 use the same projection operator; the real distinction is whether
the tangent vectors carried through =RiemannianLM= are stored in the
ambient =(n,k)= matrix shape (with a near-zero gauge subspace) or in
horizontal-basis coordinates (no gauge directions).  This naming
should be cleaned up before §2.1 is presented to a downstream user
porting code from pymanopt.

** Probe B (HIGH severity): arithmetic inconsistency between Phase 2
   dimension and Phase 5 expected rank.

Phase 2 (line 307--308) defines =manifold.dimension = k*n - k*(k-1)/2=
(the quotient dimension), and =gauge_dim = k*(k-1)/2= (line 310).

Phase 5 (line 514--516) asserts that =rank(Sigma_theta_arr)= must
equal =manifold.dimension - manifold.gauge_dim=, which evaluates to
=kn - k(k-1)/2 - k(k-1)/2 = kn - k(k-1)=.  /That double-counts the
gauge subtraction./  Either:

1. =manifold.dimension= is the /ambient/ =nk= (and Phase 2's line 307
   is wrong), in which case rank of =Sigma_theta_arr= is
   =nk - k(k-1)/2= which equals the /quotient/ dimension (good); or
2. =manifold.dimension= is the /quotient/ =kn - k(k-1)/2= (and
   Phase 5's line 516 is wrong; rank should be /equal to/
   =manifold.dimension=, not minus =gauge_dim=); or
3. The plan needs an explicit =ambient_dimension= field separate from
   =manifold.dimension=, with Phase 5 explicit about which one is the
   rank target.

This is a /spec-revision/ ask: §2.1's recommendation needs to commit
to one storage convention (ambient =nk= storage vs. quotient =kn-
k(k-1)/2= storage), and Phase 2/Phase 5 must be made consistent with
that choice.

Phase 5 line 503--507 says "Flatten the per-leaf tangent Jacobians
into one =(M, manifold.dimension)= matrix... for =PSDFixedRank(n, k)=
an ambient-space flatten (since the embedded metric matches Frobenius)
".  /Ambient-space flatten gives =nk= columns, not =kn - k(k-1)/2=./
This contradicts Phase 2 line 307.

** Probe C (MEDIUM severity): gauge ratio grows with K relative to m

For =PSDFixedRank(m, K)= the gauge fraction is
=k(k-1)/2 / (kn - k(k-1)/2)=.  Concrete numbers for K-Aggregators
parameters (=m=5= from =tmp/cereal_data.py:42=, =MAX_K=3= from the
=Makefile=):

| K | dim_quotient | gauge_dim | gauge/quotient |
|---+--------------+-----------+----------------|
| 1 |            5 |         0 |              0 |
| 2 |            9 |         1 |             11% |
| 3 |           12 |         3 |             25% |
| 4 |           14 |         6 |             43% |
| 5 |           15 |        10 |             67% |

At K-Agg's typical MAX_K=3 the gauge is 25% of the quotient
dimension, which the embedded-metric rcond threshold can probably
separate from genuine rank-deficient identification.  /But/ users who
push to K=4 or K=5 (which K-Agg's =blp_rank_analysis.py:200= advertises
as a comparison point) will hit a regime where the gauge dominates and
=cond(info_matrix)= is unreliable as a rank-deficiency diagnostic.
The plan's =gauge_nullspace_dim= field on =EstimationResult= (Phase 6)
is the right escape hatch, but the §2.1 recommendation should /flag/
this scaling explicitly --- "we recommend embedded metric for K
materially less than m; at K \approx m the quotient route or an
explicit horizontal-basis change should be the user's escape hatch".

** Probe D: pymanopt's "embedded" is already quotient-aware

Pymanopt's =projection= IS the horizontal projection.  When the plan
says (line 84) "the quotient route adds a Lyapunov solve at every step
of =RiemannianLM=", that Lyapunov solve is already in the embedded
proposal --- every retraction step requires =projection= to compute the
Riemannian gradient.  The cost differential between the two options is
not what the plan claims.

** Verdict: §2.1 needs spec revision

The decision /direction/ survives (embedded-style storage matches
pymanopt; K-Agg's gauge filter is an established pattern), but:

- the arithmetic in Phase 2 vs Phase 5 must be made consistent;
- the "embedded vs quotient" framing should be re-cast as "ambient
  =(n,k)= storage with gauge nullspace" vs. "horizontal-basis storage
  without gauge nullspace";
- the K~m regime should be flagged in the Phase 6 docstring.

* §2.2 Tangent inner product per-manifold method-defined --- SURVIVES

** Probe A: from_pymanopt interaction with Product

=Product= sums per-factor inner products (line 98).  If one factor is
=from_pymanopt(...)= (Phase 7, lines 608--631), that factor's
=inner_product= is non-JIT (line 624).  Therefore =Product=' s
combined =inner_product= is non-JIT.  This is implicit in the plan but
not flagged in §2.2 itself.  It is, however, flagged in §2.4's
acceptance scope ("OK to drop JIT for =from_pymanopt= adapters"), so
the user can recover.  Not a blocker.

** Probe B: composition with §2.1's storage choice

If §2.1 lands "ambient storage with gauge nullspace", then for
=PSDFixedRank= the =inner_product= is Frobenius on the full =(n,k)=
matrix, which is fine.  If §2.1 lands "horizontal-basis storage", the
=inner_product= must be Frobenius on the horizontal-basis coordinates,
which requires a basis transformation (computed once per call to
=projection=).  §2.2 does not flag this cross-decision dependency.

** Probe C: future Stiefel factor doesn't compose

The Riemannian metric on Stiefel can be canonical (Stiefel inner
product) or embedded (Frobenius restricted to tangent space) --- the
two are not equivalent.  If a future Product factor is Stiefel via
=from_pymanopt=, and pymanopt's default is one but the user wanted the
other, the per-factor =inner_product= method correctly delegates, but
the gradient transformations on adjacent factors don't see each other.
For =Sigma_theta=, the per-factor inner products go into a block
diagonal Gram matrix; the block diagonal structure is preserved.  This
is fine.

** Verdict: §2.2 decision survives probe

The "per-manifold method-defined" rule is the standard Riemannian
convention (Boumal §3) and the JAX-PyTree default for unspecified
subclasses is a clean fallback.  No surviving refutation.

* §2.3 =Sigma_theta= in tangent-space coordinates --- SURVIVES WITH CONCERNS

** Probe A (MEDIUM severity): tangent-coord label format inconsistency

Phase 6 (lines 562--567) shows the label format
=["L_t_00", "L_t_01", ..., "mu_t_0", "sigma_t_0", "sigma_t_1"]=.
These are /ambient indices/ ((i, j) on an (m, K) matrix), with
=manifold.dimension = nk - k(k-1)/2= and =nk= labels.  Two of every
=k(k-1)/2= labels per leaf are linear combinations (not basis
elements); the user reading "L_t_00 = 0.123 (SE 0.05)" cannot
interpret it as the standard error of L[0,0] without knowing the
horizontal basis transformation.

Compare K-Aggregators's =cereal.org:2576-2579= which uses the same
ambient =Xi[i,j]= naming.  The K-Agg pattern returns a /singular/
covariance matrix with the gauge nullspace explicitly present, not a
quotient-coordinate covariance.

If §2.3's recommendation is genuinely "tangent-space coordinates",
then the label format must use a horizontal-basis convention
(=L_t_horiz_0=, ..., =L_t_horiz_{kn-k(k-1)/2-1}=), not =(i, j)= ambient
indices.  If the plan instead intends "ambient with gauge nullspace
present", then the recommendation should be §2.3 option (2) not option
(1), and the labels match.

This is the same ambiguity as Probe B in §2.1; it needs to be resolved
in tandem.

** Probe B (MEDIUM severity): bootstrap (Section 13.4) compatibility

The asymptotic =Sigma_theta= lives in the tangent space at
=theta_hat=.  A bootstrap replicate produces a new =theta_hat^(b)=
on the manifold.  To form an empirical covariance, one needs to map
each =theta_hat^(b)= into a common chart, which on a quotient
manifold like =PSDFixedRank= requires choosing a representative of the
equivalence class (e.g., =theta_hat^(b)= rotated so =theta_hat^{(b)T}
@ theta_hat= is symmetric).  /This alignment step is not in the plan./

The =Sigma_theta= in tangent coords at =theta_hat= can be compared to
the empirical bootstrap covariance only after this alignment, plus a
=Log_{theta_hat}(theta_hat^{(b)})= step.  None of this is in the
plan's Phase 5/6 or §13.4 (which just worries about pinv-via-vmap).

For users porting from frequentist intuition --- and the plan flags
this explicitly in §13.2 (lines 770--782) --- the SE interval has to
be read as "geodesic dispersion in a particular tangent direction".
Phase 6 needs an explicit docstring about which space the SE lives in,
not just a labelled axis.

** Probe C: §2.3 option (3) (pull-back to YY^T) defers correctly

The deferral of option (3) is correct: K-Agg's
=cov_for_B_from_tangent= (=cereal.org:2581=) shows the pattern is
implementable as a delta-method post-processor, not a core engine
change.  v2.1 is the right place for it.  No refutation.

** Verdict: §2.3 decision survives but needs labels and bootstrap clarification

Option (1) (tangent-space coordinates) is the right /default/, but
the Phase 6 label format contradicts it (or the §2.1 storage choice
is implicitly option-2-style and §2.3 should match).  Bootstrap
interaction needs documentation, not implementation, before v2 ships.

* §2.4 v2.0 scope: full PSDFixedRank --- SURVIVES

** Probe A: partial PSDFixedRank (m=k, full-rank SPD) as a different manifold

For =k = m= (e.g., PSDFixedRank(5, 5)), the manifold is full-rank
symmetric PSD; pymanopt ships this as a separate class in
=positive_definite.py= with /no gauge/.  The dimension is k(k+1)/2
in an ambient k\times k space.  Could v2.0 ship this simpler manifold
first?

For K-Aggregators: =MAX_K=3= with =m=5=, so K < m always.  Full-rank
SPD wouldn't cover the K-Agg use case.  Not a refutation in the
context of the named v2 acceptance target.

For future downstream consumers (e.g., Cholesky-factor inference on a
covariance estimator), a full-rank SPD manifold is a clean
intermediate.  But this is a v3+ candidate, not a v2.0 scope question.

** Probe B: "delaying PSDFixedRank doesn't reduce Phase 1/3/4 risk"

The recommendation claim (line 146) is that Phase 2 (PSDFixedRank) is
the only numerical-risk Phase.  /False:/ Phase 4 (=RiemannianLM=) has
non-trivial numerical risk independently:

- The LM damping schedule (line 437--439) is a tuning choice;
- the Gauss-Newton solve in tangent space (line 418--419) requires a
  Gram-matrix-aware least-squares which JAX doesn't have natively;
- the convergence criterion (line 440) requires a manifold-norm
  evaluation that involves =projection= internally (gauge subtlety
  again).

The "delay PSDFixedRank to v2.1" alternative would still leave Phase 4
risky.  But the converse argument --- "shipping PSDFixedRank in v2.0
is essentially shipping all of v2 at once" --- is also true.  The
"v2.0 = Euclidean only" alternative is genuinely lower-risk; the
plan's argument for full v2.0 boils down to "K-Aggregators is blocked
otherwise".

If K-Aggregators's =MAX_K=3= production runs are not unblocked by
emu-gmm in the next sprint, the lower-risk v2.0=Euclidean path
becomes attractive.  Otherwise the plan's recommendation stands.

** Verdict: §2.4 decision survives probe

The argument from acceptance target (K-Agg is blocked) is the
load-bearing one, and it is /currently/ true.  If K-Agg gets unblocked
by another path, this decision should be revisited.
