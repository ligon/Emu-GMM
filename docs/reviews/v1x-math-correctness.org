* Lens

Adversarial review of the v1.x =src/emu_gmm/= implementation against
textbook GMM formulas. References:

- Hansen (1982), Econometrica 50:1029-54.
- Hayashi (2000), /Econometrics/, Ch. 3.
- Cameron & Miller (2015), J. Human Resources 50:317-72.
- Kleibergen (2005), Econometrica 73:1103-23.
- van der Vaart, /Asymptotic Statistics/, §5.

* Summary verdict

=concerns=. The core estimator math is correct and matches textbook
formulas: Cholesky path, whitening, Sigma_theta via G' Lambda G,
IIDCovariance, ClusteredCovariance, and SyntheticCovariance all
reproduce reference computations to floating-point tolerance on toy
inputs. The CU gradient correctly captures =nabla_theta V= via JAX AD
through the Cholesky and forward-solve. The framework's "no external
N multiplier" convention is verified: J converges cleanly to
=chi^2_M= on a Monte Carlo experiment.

The principal concern is /not/ wrong math but a /policy/ discrepancy:
the design's "anchor-once-then-freeze" tau policy
(=design.org= §5; =CLAUDE.md= commitment #3) is /not/ implemented in
v1. =DiagonalTikhonov.apply= is called inside the residual closure at
every theta evaluation, and the bisection contains a
=jnp.where(kappa <= kappa_target, 0.0, tau_search)= short-circuit that
makes =tau= a discontinuous function of theta where the condition
crosses the target. This breaks the smoothness of the objective
surface that LM / quasi-Newton solvers rely on.

A second, milder concern: the centering convention is inconsistent
across the covariance ladder. =IIDCovariance= and
=ClusteredCovariance= use the uncentered Hansen outer-product form
=sum psi_j psi_k=, while =SyntheticCovariance= and the
=AnalyticalCovariance= contract use the centered population-variance
form =E[psi psi'] - E[psi] E[psi]'=. The two coincide at theta_0
(where E[psi] = 0 by the moment condition), so the asymptotic claims
in =mcar-asymptotics.org= still go through, but off-optimum V (and
hence the CU surface away from the optimum) differs from a centered
counterpart.

* Findings

** [HIGH] Anchor-once-then-freeze tau policy NOT implemented in v1

- /Where/: =src/emu_gmm/estimator.py:188= calls
  =V_star, _tau = regularization.apply(V)= /inside/ the residual
  closure =residual_fn(theta_flat)=, so =tau= is re-computed at every
  theta evaluation during optimisation.
- /What design.org §5 specifies/ (lines 285-287):
  "tau is selected /once/ at an anchor point (first-stage theta_1 for
  =Fixed= / =Iterated=; a pilot evaluation for =ContinuouslyUpdated=)
  /and held fixed thereafter/."
- /What =CLAUDE.md= commitment #3 specifies/:
  "the anchor-once-then-freeze policy keeps the objective smooth."
- /Smoothness consequence/: =DiagonalTikhonov.apply=
  (=regularization.py:122-124=) contains
  =tau = jnp.where(already_ok, jnp.asarray(0.0), tau_search)=. As
  theta moves and =kappa(V(theta))= crosses =kappa_target=, =tau=
  jumps from a positive value to zero (or vice versa). The resulting
  =V_star(theta) = V(theta) + tau(theta) diag(V(theta))= is
  discontinuous, hence so is =L(theta)= and the residual =y(theta)=.
- /Risk/: Levenberg-Marquardt and other Jacobian-based optimisers
  assume a smooth residual; a discontinuity in =tau= produces
  spurious convergence behaviour, possibly silent failure to find
  the true minimum.
- /Reference/: Hansen (1982) §3 (assumption of continuity of the
  weighting); van der Vaart §5.21 (smoothness conditions for the
  delta-method argument that yields asymptotic normality).
- /Fix sketch/: introduce a separate phase in =estimate()= that
  computes =V0 = covariance(model, theta_init, measure)=,
  =V0_star, tau_anchor = regularization.apply(V0)=, then constructs a
  residual closure that uses
  =V_star = V(theta) + tau_anchor * diag(V(theta))= /without/
  recomputing tau at each theta. Pass =tau_anchor= through as a
  static / frozen quantity.

** [MEDIUM] Centering convention inconsistent across covariance strategies

- /Where/:
  - =covariance/iid.py:122= and =covariance/clustered.py:143= use
    /uncentered/ outer products of =psi= values: =sum_i psi_j psi_k=.
  - =covariance/synthetic.py:84-89= uses /centered/ outer products:
    =sum_s (psi_s - mbar)(psi_s - mbar)'=.
  - =design.org= §2 line 128 specifies the centered form for
    =AnalyticalCovariance= (= ∫ psi psi' f - E[psi] E[psi]'); the
    user-supplied =covariance_fn= is responsible.
- /Why it's defensible/: at theta_0 the moment condition forces
  E[psi(theta_0)] = 0, so centered and uncentered agree
  asymptotically. Hansen (1982) §3.2 explicitly uses the uncentered
  outer-product form for the optimal-weight matrix estimator. The
  MCAR consistency theorem in =docs/mcar-asymptotics.org= Theorem 3
  is stated and proved for the uncentered form.
- /Why it's still a concern/: off-optimum (during optimisation) the
  two differ by =mbar mbar'=, which is /not/ asymptotically negligible
  at arbitrary theta. The CU objective therefore uses a different
  surface from a centred counterpart at non-zero =m(theta)=. The
  /resulting/ optimum is the same, but the /path/ and the
  /finite-sample CU gradient/ at off-optimum theta differ.
- /Reference/: Hansen (1982) Lemma 4.1 uses uncentered moments;
  Hayashi (2000) §3.5 discusses both conventions and notes the
  off-optimum discrepancy.
- /Action/: either (a) document the off-optimum discrepancy
  prominently in =design.org= §2 alongside the IIDCovariance formula,
  or (b) provide a =CenteredIIDCovariance= variant for users who
  want the centred form. Not a bug; a convention-documentation gap.

** [LOW] SyntheticCovariance docstring says "n^2 form" without explaining the convention

- /Where/: =covariance/synthetic.py:20-22=:
  "This is the biased / maximum-likelihood form (denominator n^2)
  rather than the unbiased n(n-1) form."
- /Issue/: the wording elides which quantity has the n^2 denominator.
  The standard "biased vs unbiased" distinction refers to the /sample
  variance of psi/ (denominator n vs n-1), not to the /variance of
  the sample mean/ (which gets an extra 1/n). The two-step reasoning
  ("sample variance with 1/n; divide by n for variance of mean")
  yields n^2 in the denominator, which is what the code does, but
  the docstring obscures this.
- /Action/: clarify the docstring; the n^2 comes from
  =(sample variance) / n = (1/n) * (1/n) sum (psi-mbar)^2=.

** [LOW] Quadratic-form derivation in design.org Section 4 is correct but does not match the implementation route

- /Where/: =design.org= §4 line 241 derives
  =nabla Q = 2 G' V^{-1} m - m' V^{-1} (nabla V) V^{-1} m=.
- /Implementation route/: The estimator constructs the residual
  =y(theta) = L(theta)^{-1} m(theta)= and lets the LM solver compute
  =J^T y= with =J = dy/dtheta= via JAX AD. The full =nabla Q= falls
  out automatically because LM forms =2 J^T y= for the gradient of
  =||y||^2=.
- /Sanity/: numerical check confirms the residual-path gradient
  agrees with the direct =jax.grad(Q)= to floating-point tolerance.
- /Action/: optional cross-reference in =design.org= §5 noting the
  LM residual route delivers the §4 gradient automatically.
