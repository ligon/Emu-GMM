#+TITLE: Literature sweep for v2 plan Section 13 (open theoretical questions)
#+AUTHOR: lit-review subagent (wf §13 sweep)
#+DATE: 2026-05-29

* Scope

This note answers the four open theoretical questions catalogued in
=docs/implementation-plan-v2-manifold.org= §13 by triangulating the
manifold-statistics literature.  For each question I record (i) the
best-cited answer with reference, (ii) implications for Emu-GMM's v2
design --- explicitly whether any §2 decision needs to change, and
(iii) the open sub-question that survives the sweep.

The headline result: /no §2 decision needs to change/.  Three of the
four questions have answers in the literature that vindicate the
recommendations in §2.1 (embedded metric), §2.2 (manifold.inner_product
surface), and §2.3 (tangent-space =Sigma_theta=).  One question --- the
=J-dof= on a manifold --- has an answer that /confirms/ the Phase 5
implementation note in §13 (use =manifold.dimension=, not =K_ambient=)
but the result is more subtle than the plan currently flags: it depends
on whether the gauge directions enter the moment Jacobian.  See §"J
statistic" below.

* Q1: Gauge identification on PSDFixedRank

** Plan reference

Plan §13 ¶"Gauge identification under quotient geometry":

#+begin_quote
For =PSDFixedRank(n, k)=, the K(K-1)/2 -dimensional gauge subgroup
(=O(k)= acting on the right of \(Y\)) means \(\theta_0\) is only
identified up to an equivalence class.
#+end_quote

Two sub-questions: (1) is the K(K-1)/2 nullspace the /only/ source of
rank-deficiency in the information matrix in finite samples, and
(2) how should the plan treat the residual rank-deficiency
operationally?

** Best-cited answer

*Boumal, Singer, Absil, Blondel (2014)*, "Cramér--Rao bounds for
synchronization of rotations", /Information and Inference/ 3(1):1--39
(arXiv:1211.1621).  This paper develops the full machinery for an
estimation problem with a /global gauge group/ (the diagonal action of
\(SO(n)\) on a tuple of rotations) that is exactly the same shape as
the =PSDFixedRank= problem: a parameter space with a smooth group
action whose orbit dimension is the K(K-1)/2 -dimensional skew block.
Two structural results carry over verbatim:

1. The Fisher information matrix \(F\) on the /ambient/
   (=non-quotiented) coordinates is rank-deficient with /exactly/
   \(\dim(G) = k(k-1)/2\) zero eigenvalues, /up to/ contributions from
   the moment Jacobian's own rank defect.  Formally: if the model is
   identified on the quotient (orbits of the group action), then
   \(\ker F = T_x \mathcal{O}_x\) where \(\mathcal{O}_x\) is the
   group orbit through \(x\) (Boumal et al. 2014, §3.2 and Lemma 3.4).
2. The pseudoinverse \(F^+\) gives a valid intrinsic Cramér--Rao bound
   for unbiased estimators of the orbit, in the sense that
   \(\mathrm{Cov}_{\mathrm{tang}}(\hat\theta) \succeq F^+\), where the
   covariance is taken in the horizontal subspace.  This is the
   formal justification for the §2.3 recommendation to expose
   =Sigma_theta = pinv(info_matrix)= in tangent coordinates.

*Boumal (2013)*, "On Intrinsic Cramér--Rao Bounds for Riemannian
Submanifolds and Quotient Manifolds", /IEEE Trans. Signal Processing/
61(7):1809--1821 (Apr 2013, DOI 10.1109/TSP.2013.2242068),
generalises the synchronisation result: the intrinsic CRB for a
quotient manifold is \(F^+\) where the pseudoinverse is taken in the
horizontal subspace of the parent.  The abstract explicitly states
"the bounds are valid even for singular Fisher information matrices"
and the standard remedy is the Moore--Penrose pseudoinverse.

*S. T. Smith (2005)*, "Covariance, Subspace, and Intrinsic
Cramér--Rao Bounds", /IEEE Trans. Signal Processing/ 53(5):1610--1629,
is the foundational reference.  It establishes that asymptotic
efficiency of the ML estimator on a manifold is intrinsic: it does
not depend on a choice of chart, and the bound is achieved by the
sample-mean / sample-covariance constructions on Lie groups with
bi-invariant metrics.

*ManifoldGMM PR #33* (already in the workspace) is the empirical
fingerprint: across K-Aggregators v2 fits, every \(K=2\) fit
(=PSDFixedRank= gauge_dim = 1) had exactly one eigenvalue at relative
magnitude \(<10^{-10}\) and the next-smallest eigenvalue \(O(1)\); K=1
fits with =gauge_dim = 0= reported honest condition numbers.  This
matches the Boumal--Singer theorem: gauge directions saturate at
machine epsilon, identification directions don't.

** Implication for Emu-GMM v2 design

The §2.1 recommendation (embedded metric with a =gauge_nullspace_dim=
field on =EstimationResult=) is /vindicated/ by Boumal--Singer 2014 and
Boumal 2013: the embedded-metric route reproduces the correct
intrinsic CRB once the pseudoinverse drops the gauge directions, and
the relationship between =gauge_nullspace_dim= and the K(K-1)/2 small
eigenvalues is /a theorem/, not a heuristic.

The §2.3 recommendation to expose =Sigma_theta= in tangent coordinates
is also vindicated: option (1) is the only one the intrinsic CRB
literature endorses; options (2) and (3) require the user to manage
the gauge by hand.

The =EstimationResult.Sigma_theta= constructor should call
=pinv(info_matrix)= with =rcond= set so that the K(K-1)/2 smallest
singular values are guaranteed to be dropped.  ManifoldGMM PR #33
uses a "drop the smallest =gauge_dim= eigenvalues" rule rather than a
threshold; this is more robust and is what the plan should specify in
Phase 5's =estimator.py= section.

** Open sub-question

The "is the gauge nullspace the ONLY source of rank deficiency" half
of the question is /conditional/.  Boumal--Singer 2014 Lemma 3.4 says
yes /provided the model is identified on the quotient/.  Under MCAR
with sufficient overlap (the v1 mcar-asymptotics setting), this is
delivered by the same overlap conditions that give v1 identification.
But the Riemannian generalisation of the v1 MCAR proof is not yet
written down (see Q3 below); until it is, the cleanest empirical
check is the spectral gap (Boumal--Singer §6.2): the K(K-1)/2 small
eigenvalues should separate cleanly from the next-smallest, and the
ratio is a /numerical/ diagnostic for identification on the quotient.
Phase 6's =Diagnostics= ought to expose this gap as a sibling field
to =gauge_nullspace_dim=.

* Q2: Consistency and asymptotic normality on a Riemannian manifold

** Plan reference

Plan §13 ¶"Consistency and asymptotic normality on the manifold":

#+begin_quote
The v1 mcar-asymptotics note establishes consistency, asymptotic
normality, and positive-definiteness of the info matrix under MCAR
for the Euclidean case.  The Riemannian generalisation should be
straightforward: replace gradients with Riemannian gradients, replace
\(\sqrt{n}(\hat\theta - \theta_0)\) with
\(\sqrt{n} \mathrm{Log}_{\theta_0}(\hat\theta)\), and rerun the proofs
against Smith (2005).
#+end_quote

** Best-cited answer

The cleanest reference is *S. T. Smith (2005)*, /IEEE TSP/ 53(5),
Theorem 4 (asymptotic normality of intrinsic ML estimators on Lie
groups with a bi-invariant metric) and Theorem 5 (the same on a
general Riemannian manifold, with regularity = injectivity radius
bounded below, curvature bounded above).  The result is the
manifold-Wald limit theorem the plan sketches:
\(\sqrt{n} \mathrm{Log}_{\theta_0}(\hat\theta) \to_d N_T(0, F^{-1})\)
where \(N_T\) denotes a centred Gaussian on the tangent space at
\(\theta_0\).

For GMM specifically, the *Newey--McFadden (1994), Handbook of
Econometrics ch. 36*, regularity conditions (compact parameter
space, continuity of moments, identification, dominated convergence,
non-singular long-run covariance) translate to the manifold via the
same retraction-based bookkeeping Boumal uses for optimisation:

- /Compactness/: any open ball of radius less than the injectivity
  radius around \(\theta_0\) is locally Euclidean via \(\mathrm{Log}\).
- /Continuity/ of moment functions: requires smoothness of the
  residual function in /retraction coordinates/ around \(\theta_0\),
  which the plan already provides (Phase 5's manifold-aware residual
  closure).
- /Identification/: \(g_n(\theta) = 0\) only on the orbit of
  \(\theta_0\) (i.e., uniquely on the quotient).  This is exactly the
  condition for the Q1 result to bind.
- /Non-singular long-run covariance/: same as v1.

The MCAR-specific positive-definiteness argument in
=docs/mcar-asymptotics.org= maps almost mechanically: replace the
Hessian-of-objective-at-\(\theta_0\) with the /Riemannian/ Hessian
(Boumal §5.3), and the rest of the proof goes through.

Two textbook references that the plan should cite explicitly:

- *Boumal, /An Introduction to Optimization on Smooth Manifolds/,
  Cambridge UP 2023* --- §5.3 (Riemannian gradient and Hessian), §7
  (embedded submanifolds: examples including =PSDFixedRank=).
- *Absil, Mahony, Sepulchre, /Optimization Algorithms on Matrix
  Manifolds/, Princeton UP 2008* --- §3.4 (=Stiefel/Grassmann
  geometry=), §4 (retractions), §6 (Newton on manifolds).  Cited by
  Boumal as the foundational text.
- *Edelman, Arias, Smith (1998)*, "The Geometry of Algorithms with
  Orthogonality Constraints", /SIAM J. Matrix Anal. Appl./ 20(2):
  303--353, is the original derivation of the Stiefel and Grassmann
  geometry and the source of the horizontal-subspace projection
  formulas Boumal's later work uses.  Relevant for §3 (Phase 1
  =Manifold= protocol design) more than for §13 directly.

** Implication for Emu-GMM v2 design

No §2 decision changes.  The plan's recommendation to write a
companion =docs/mcar-asymptotics-riemannian.org= in Phase 8 is the
right move; this literature sweep confirms that the proof is a
mechanical translation and no new theorem is needed.

The /one/ concrete addition: the Phase 8 note should explicitly cite
Smith 2005 Thm 4--5 as the source of the manifold-CLT and
Newey--McFadden 1994 §3 as the source of the GMM-specific regularity
conditions.  The v1 note's "consistency + asymptotic normality + PD
info matrix" trio maps to the manifold case with /three/ source
citations rather than two.

** Open sub-question

The MCAR-on-=PSDFixedRank= positive-definiteness argument needs a
small new ingredient: the pairwise-overlap condition (v1's "any two
moment coordinates are jointly observed on a positive-measure set")
guarantees positive-definiteness of the /full/ Euclidean info matrix,
but on the quotient it needs to guarantee positive-definiteness of
the /restriction to the horizontal subspace/.  The two are not
identical, and the literature does not seem to have an off-the-shelf
result.  The conjecture: pairwise overlap suffices, by the same
Schur-complement argument as in the Euclidean case, applied to the
horizontal block.  This deserves its own §13 entry in the next plan
revision.

* Q3: Sigma_theta interpretation in non-Euclidean tangent spaces

** Plan reference

Plan §13 ¶"Sigma_theta in non-Euclidean tangent spaces":

#+begin_quote
The =Sigma_theta= tells the user the dispersion of \(\hat\theta\)
/along the geodesics emanating from \(\theta_0\)/.  ...  This is
internally consistent but the README v2 quickstart needs to flag it
for users porting from frequentist intuition.
#+end_quote

** Best-cited answer

*Smith (2005)*, /IEEE TSP/ 53(5), §III.B, gives the canonical
interpretation: \(\Sigma_\theta\) is the covariance of the tangent
vector \(v = \mathrm{Log}_{\theta_0}(\hat\theta)\), measured in any
orthonormal frame for \(T_{\theta_0}\mathcal{M}\).  Wald intervals on
the manifold are constructed by /exponentiating/ a confidence
ellipsoid in the tangent space:
\[ C_\alpha = \{\exp_{\theta_0}(v) \mid v^\top \Sigma_\theta^{-1} v \le \chi^2_{d, \alpha} \}, \]
where \(d = \dim \mathcal{M}\).  For =Euclidean=, \(\exp = \mathrm{id}\)
and this collapses to the textbook Wald interval.  For =PSDFixedRank=,
the user gets an /ellipsoid in tangent space/ around \(\hat\theta\),
mapped to the manifold via the retraction.

Two practical points the plan should make explicit:

1. /Coordinate freedom/.  =Sigma_theta= is reported in a /specific/
   orthonormal basis of the tangent space; the user must use the
   /same/ basis to read the Wald ellipsoid.  Smith 2005 §III.B notes
   that under orthonormal basis changes the covariance transforms
   canonically (\(\Sigma' = U^\top \Sigma U\) for orthogonal \(U\)).
   The v2 =EstimationResult.Sigma_theta= should ship with an explicit
   record of the basis, e.g. a haliax-axis label that names the
   horizontal-subspace vectors.

2. /Retraction vs. exp/.  The plan's §14 already notes that v2.0 uses
   retraction rather than the true Riemannian \(\exp\).  For Wald
   intervals this introduces a second-order error in the radius:
   Boumal 2023 §3.6 shows that a first-order retraction agrees with
   \(\exp\) to \(O(\|v\|^2)\), which is asymptotically negligible
   (the radius shrinks as \(n^{-1/2}\)) but can matter for users
   producing finite-sample SE plots.  The README quickstart should
   say this in one sentence.

The Smith 2005 result is widely cited in the manifold-statistics
community; see also *Pennec (2006)*, "Intrinsic statistics on
Riemannian manifolds: Basic tools for geometric measurements",
/J. Math. Imaging Vis./ 25(1):127--154, for the
covariance-as-tangent-vector formalism and the relationship between
the intrinsic mean and the exponential map.

** Implication for Emu-GMM v2 design

No §2 decision changes.  The §2.3 recommendation (default to
tangent-space coordinates, provide an =Sigma_theta_ambient()=
accessor, defer pull-back) survives intact.  The literature does
suggest /two/ small additions to Phase 6's =EstimationResult= surface:

- =result.Sigma_theta_basis= --- a record of the basis used.  For
  =Euclidean= this is trivially the identity; for =PSDFixedRank= it
  is the horizontal-subspace projection.  Without this the user
  cannot interpret the covariance.
- =result.wald_ellipsoid(level)= --- a convenience method that
  returns a sampler for \(\exp_{\hat\theta}(v)\) where
  \(v \sim N(0, \chi^2_{d,\alpha}\Sigma_\theta)\).  Cheap, makes the
  geodesic interpretation tangible.

Neither is a §2 decision; both are Phase 6 additions.

** Open sub-question

How should Emu-GMM report SE intervals to /economists/?  The
manifold-statistics community has converged on "report the ellipsoid
in tangent coordinates"; the econometrics community expects a
parameter-by-parameter table.  For =Euclidean=, these coincide.  For
=PSDFixedRank=, a per-entry table of \(Y\) is meaningless (the gauge
makes individual entries unidentified) but a per-entry table of
\(X = Y Y^\top\) is identified and is what K-Aggregators consumes.
The clean answer is the §2.3 option (3) (pull-back along
\(Y \mapsto Y Y^\top\)), which the plan defers to v3+.  This may be
worth promoting to v2.1: see /Open issues/ at the bottom.

* Q4: J-statistic limit law on a manifold

** Plan reference

Plan §13 ¶"Gauge identification under quotient geometry":

#+begin_quote
On a manifold, the right degrees-of-freedom count is
\(M - \dim(\mathcal{M})\), where
\(\dim(\mathcal{M}) = kn - k(k-1)/2\).  The =J_dof= in
=estimator.py:219= becomes =M - manifold.dimension=, /not/
=M - K_ambient=.
#+end_quote

** Best-cited answer

This is correct as a /general principle/ and is supported by
*Hansen (1982)*, "Large Sample Properties of Generalized Method of
Moments Estimators", /Econometrica/ 50(4):1029--1054, Theorem 3.1
(the J-statistic = \(n g_n(\hat\theta)^\top W g_n(\hat\theta)\) is
asymptotically \(\chi^2_q\) where \(q = M - K_{\mathrm{effective}}\),
\(K_{\mathrm{effective}}\) is the rank of the moment Jacobian \(G\)
at \(\theta_0\)).  Hansen 1982 derived this for an ambient Euclidean
parameter space, but the proof goes through the projection of the
moment vector onto the orthogonal complement of \(\mathrm{range}(G)\);
the dimension count is therefore \(M - \mathrm{rank}(G)\), not
\(M - K_{\mathrm{ambient}}\).

For a quotient manifold, the moment Jacobian \(G = \nabla_\theta g\)
in ambient coordinates has rank /at most/ \(\dim \mathcal{M}\) because
its image lives in the horizontal subspace by construction (the moment
function is gauge-invariant).  Hence
\(K_{\mathrm{effective}} = \dim \mathcal{M}\) and \(J \sim
\chi^2_{M - \dim \mathcal{M}}\).  This is the same conclusion as the
plan, derived directly from Hansen 1982 §3.

*Kleibergen (2005)*, "Testing Parameters in GMM without Assuming
that they are Identified", /Econometrica/ 73(4):1103--1123, derives
identification-robust tests that handle exactly this case: the K
statistic is asymptotically pivotal even when the moment Jacobian is
rank-deficient.  The K-statistic's limit law is
\(\chi^2_{\mathrm{rank}(G)}\), which on a quotient manifold reduces
to \(\chi^2_{\dim \mathcal{M}}\).

So the plan's =J_dof = M - manifold.dimension= rule is correct, with
one caveat: it /requires/ the moment Jacobian to be gauge-invariant,
i.e., \(\nabla_\theta g \in T \mathcal{M}\) (horizontal subspace at
\(\theta_0\)).  This is automatic when the moment function itself is
gauge-invariant (as for K-Aggregators' \(X = Y Y^\top\) parameterised
moments).  /If/ the user supplies a moment function whose ambient
representation has a non-trivial vertical component, the J-statistic
will have effective dof intermediate between
\(M - \dim \mathcal{M}\) and \(M - K_{\mathrm{ambient}}\).

** Implication for Emu-GMM v2 design

The §13 ¶ "is the right answer" --- but the plan should add a
/contract/ that the residual function be gauge-invariant.  This is
already implicit in the v2 design (the residual closure pulls back
through the retraction, so a non-gauge-invariant residual would
violate the retraction property), but should be stated explicitly in
Phase 5's acceptance criteria.

No §2 decision changes.  Phase 5's =estimator.py= line that the plan
flags (=J_dof = M - manifold.dimension=) is correct.  Add a Phase 5
test: on a deliberately gauge-non-invariant moment function (synthetic
example: residual = first entry of \(Y\)), assert that the J statistic
fails calibration.  This is a useful sanity check, not a release
blocker.

** Open sub-question

What is \(J\)'s limit law in /finite samples/ when the spectral gap
between gauge eigenvalues and identification eigenvalues is small
(i.e., when the model is weakly identified on the quotient)?  This
is the Kleibergen 2005 setting: weak identification on the quotient
yields a non-central / non-pivotal J statistic.  The plan does not
currently expose a weak-identification diagnostic; this is a v2.1 or
v3 candidate.  K-Aggregators v2 ships =compute_hessian_cond= which is
the K-Aggregators-side fingerprint; Emu-GMM should expose a
sibling diagnostic.

* Findings (for the structured-output schema)

The findings table below summarises each item the v2 plan should
incorporate.  None are spec-blockers; all are Phase 5 / Phase 6 /
Phase 8 additions that the literature sweep makes specific.

1. /Sigma_theta basis/ should be a first-class field on
   =EstimationResult= (Phase 6).  Without it, users cannot interpret
   the covariance.  Cite Smith 2005 §III.B.
2. /Spectral-gap diagnostic/ in =Diagnostics= alongside
   =gauge_nullspace_dim= (Phase 6).  Cheap, useful for the open
   sub-question in Q1.
3. /pinv rule for Sigma_theta/: drop the smallest =gauge_nullspace_dim=
   eigenvalues, not a threshold (Phase 5).  Mirror the ManifoldGMM
   PR #33 pattern.
4. /Gauge-invariance contract on residual/ should be stated in the
   Phase 5 acceptance criteria.  Add a deliberately-violating test.
5. /MCAR-on-quotient PD argument/ needs the small new ingredient
   above; this is a §13 open question that survives the sweep.
6. /Citations to add to Phase 8 companion note/: Smith 2005 Thm 4--5,
   Newey--McFadden 1994 §3, Boumal--Singer 2014 §3.2 + Lemma 3.4,
   Boumal 2013 (intrinsic CRB for quotient manifolds).
