* Scope

The Arc-2 exit criterion (issue #130): repeated-sampling evidence of
size, Wald coverage, and J-calibration for the covariance ladder ---
=IIDCovariance= -> =ClusteredCovariance= -> =StratifiedCovariance= ->
=DesignAwareCovariance= --- under genuine per-coordinate missingness,
produced by the supported =emu_gmm.studies= driver. Companion to the
algebra-level tests that landed with #79/#80/#109/#111/#119; this
report adds the distributional half. Two owner-approved columns ride
along: tau-binding frequency, and CU+LM robustness under misspecified
alternatives (the #9 reopen-decision evidence).

Harness: =scripts/validation/ladder_mc.py= (DGP family, study registry,
runnable commands recorded per study below). Adversarial design review:
the harness draft went through a 5-lens adversarial workflow
(falsifiability, DGP-vs-estimand correctness, CRN protocol, numerics,
checklist coverage) with 2-skeptic refutation before the full runs;
surviving findings and their dispositions are in the appendix.

* The DGP family (fixture-engineering notes)

See the harness module docstring for the full spec. Headlines:

- Stratified PSU-randomized experiment: S strata x 2 arms x G_c PSUs of
  m units; T assigned at PSU level within stratum.
- Intracluster correlation rho at PSU level (the IID-vs-clustered
  discriminator); FIXED demeaned stratum profile sigma_strat (the
  centered-vs-uncentered discriminator --- see the DesignSpec docstring
  for why a per-replicate stratum *random* effect would be a fixture
  bug, caught in piloting).
- M = 4 moments r*(1, T, x, w), K = 2, J_dof = 2; the x and w moments
  observable per PSU with probabilities (p_x, p_w) --- genuine unequal
  N_j, pair-specific overlap, and H_{c,jk} < 2 boundary cells at small
  G_c.
- Misspecification knob delta violates only E[r w] = 0.
- Calibration arms start at the truth and the affected studies anchor
  the ridge PER REPLICATE (see "Protocol findings" below and emu-gmm
  #142); the misspec study starts far (theta_init = (0,0)) by design.

* Protocol findings surfaced by piloting

These were found while engineering the fixtures (30-50 rep pilots) and
shaped the harness; recorded because they are evidence in their own
right:

- *Factory-shared ridge anchor* (-> emu-gmm #142): =build_estimator=
  freezes tau_anchor (and binding_ridge) on the template draw, so
  driver-replicated studies inherit replicate 0's anchor: the
  tau-binding column degenerates to {0,1} per arm, one unlucky template
  poisons an arm (pilot: coupled design-aware, tau-bind 1.000, 57%
  non-convergence from a far start), one lucky template masks per-rep
  pathology. Studies 3 and 5 therefore anchor per replicate (bare
  =estimate()= per rep).
- *Anchor-at-far-init*: anchoring V(theta_init) at (0,0) anchors on a
  wildly mis-scaled V; with the coupled design-aware assembly the
  anchor went indefinite and tau bound for the whole arm. Calibration
  arms start at truth (documented choice); optimization robustness is
  study 4's job.
- *Per-replicate stratum random effects are a fixture bug* (not a
  package bug): no within-cell-centered or PSU-clustered estimator
  targets between-stratum variance of non-contrast moments; pilot
  intercept coverage 0.17-0.73 across ALL arms. Fixed demeaned
  profiles restore exact moment conditions (and the intended
  centered-vs-uncentered discrimination).
- *Exactly-singular V is unrepairable by DiagonalTikhonov*: a fully
  unsupported moment row (all H_{c,jk} < 2 for some j) zeroes
  diag(V)_jj, so tau*diag(V) cannot lift it; reps die instead of
  binding. The binding study's observability is tuned (p_w = 0.7,
  12 cells) to make this a ~2e-4 event; the near-boundary regime is
  the interesting one.

  both scales: rho0.5/iid rejects J at 0.466 and covers at 0.41

  strategies (0.043/0.047) -- the contrast is the clustering, not the
  estimator.
- *Clustered inference at G=100 under genuine missingness:* coverage
  0.921/0.927 (~3 SE below nominal; SE/SD ~ 0.92 -- the familiar
  small-G downward SE bias, mild at this scale), J shows
  TAIL-SPECIFIC conservatism: rej 0.022-0.024 at nominal 5% (3.7 SE
  low) while the p-value ECDF is otherwise uniform (J-KS 0.017). The
  pilots' 0/80 was this conservatism, quantified.
- *Few-cluster regime (checklist item 1):* at G=12 the failure mode is
  honest and large -- SE/SD ~ 0.64-0.69 (SEs ~ 35% small), coverage
  ~ 0.80, J effectively never rejects (rej 0.000, KS 0.09-0.14).
  Practitioners in this regime need the wild-cluster bootstrap, not
  the asymptotic J.
- *dof_correction (pre-registered):* G=100 pair confirmed a no-op
  (flips +1/-0; mean dJ -0.03). At G=12 the paired contrast SURPRISED:
  flips are two-sided (+7/-11) because the per-pair G_jk/(G_jk-1)
  correction enters the CU *weighting* and moves theta_hat itself --
  the dof knob is not a pure CI-widening under CUE. The review's
  one-directional sign-test premise was wrong; the readout caveat is
  revised accordingly (post-run docstring fix, noted in Reproducibility).
  Net effect at G=12 remains small (coverage 0.792 vs 0.797).

** Study 2 --- StratifiedCovariance across PSU counts, fpc, H boundary

Claims (PRE-REGISTERED before the full runs; adversarial-review fixes
4 and 7): (a) within-cell-centered stratified V calibrated at
G in {5, 20} under the fixed-stratum-profile DGP where uncentered PSU
clustering is conservative (pools strata means); (b) the fpc arm is a

(~0.003/rep) counted as a named event. The G2hi (p_w=0.85) arm is the

** Study 3 --- DesignAware: coupling + #119 dof inheritance (per-rep anchored)

RESCOPED claims (adversarial-review fix 3): under the fixed demeaned
v_strat profile all per-rep randomness is independent across PSUs, so
the true cross-ARM component of V_TS is exactly zero in this fixture;
the couple=True/False contrast exercises the MECHANICAL same-unit
coupling only (m_T a sub-sum of m_const; Var(b1) inflates ~3x under
couple=False). A genuine cross-arm-shock fixture is recorded follow-up
work. Added arms: (i) sigma_strat=0 contamination check --- if the
coupled arm's b1 coverage normalizes without the stratum profile, the
pilot's under-coverage (SE/SD ~0.49) is profile contamination of the
uncentered V_SS/V_TS blocks glued to centered V_TT; (ii) the #119
dof-inheritance ablation (sampling dof_correction on/off) in a
few-cluster sampling regime (G=12) read via CRN-paired flips, since at
G~100 the per-pair correction is a deterministic ~1.01 no-op. The
review REJECTED switching the sampling engine to stratum-level
clustering under this DGP (uncentered stratum totals would pick up the
fixed profile means; no cross-arm term exists for it to capture).

** Study 5 --- Regularised-J calibration where the ridge ACTUALLY binds (per-rep anchored)

provably never tripped the feasibility test --- measured anchors at

z5 = x + eps*eta, with eps swept {3e-2, 1e-2, 3e-3} so the measured
binding frequency spans low-to-high; a no-eps baseline pins the
no-binding contrast. Claims: (a) the item-4 reading --- among BINDING
reps, J_pvalue_adjusted's ECDF/KS calibrates where the nominal does
not (Welch-Satterthwaite suffices; else Davies/Imhof) --- is GATED on
the measured binding frequency (the jadj readout refuses arms with ~0
binding events); (b) the near-boundary stratified V degrades (J
over-rejection, under-coverage) WITHOUT tripping the feasibility test

  mcar-asymptotics Theorem 6 describes), the nominal J p-value badly
  fails uniformity over the full law (KS 0.211, critical ~0.087 at
  n = 244) while the ADJUSTED p-value passes (KS 0.085). Binding-subset
  ECDFs are selected subsamples (conditional uniformity is not the
  null), so the full-law contrast is the decisive cell. In the
  #145-contaminated arms the adjustment neither helps nor should: the
  COVARIANCE is mis-centered there, and no reference-distribution
  correction can repair a wrong V.
- *The dangerous regime is the feasible-but-degraded one, not the
  binding one* (the #134-decisive finding): =strat_eps3e-2= over-
  rejects J at 0.314 with binding_ridge == 0 on every rep -- the
  near-collinear-but-PD V sails through the signed-spectrum
  feasibility test while badly miscalibrated, WORSE than the
  H-boundary degradation alone (=noeps_strat=, 0.221). Any emergency
  floor keyed to infeasibility is blind to this regime by
  construction.
- The amortized cache-clearing ran these arms ~4.5x faster than
  design_aware's per-rep clearing (746-869 s vs ~3500-3900 s per arm),

* Verdict

The ladder validates where its assumptions hold, fails honestly and
diagnosably where they do not, and the validation surfaced two real
defects (both now filed and one doc-mitigated) --- which is what a
validation pillar is for.

1. *Calibration delivered*: IIDCovariance under independence;
   ClusteredCovariance at moderate-to-large G (with a mild,
   quantified tail-conservatism of the J: rej ~2.2-2.4% at nominal
   5%, ECDF otherwise uniform); StratifiedCovariance at G >= 5 under
   exactly the estimand its centering targets, with every
   pre-registered guaranteed-fail row (fpc, H-boundary) reproducing
   its predicted magnitudes.
2. *Discrimination delivered*: the wrong-strategy arms fail by 7-45
   sigma at every scale tested; a study in which nothing could fail
   validates nothing, and no study here is of that kind (the
   adversarial review's falsifiability lens signed off on the final
   arm set).
3. *Honest failure regimes, quantified*: few-cluster (G ~ 12)
   cluster-robust SEs run ~35% small; the H_{c,jk} < 2 boundary
   degrades the stratified V in proportion to the boundary share;
   the feasible-but-degraded near-collinear V is the worst J
   miscalibration in the suite (rej 0.31) and is INVISIBLE to the
   feasibility test -- prefer conservative estimators or bootstrap
   inference in all three regimes.
4. *Defects surfaced by the validation*: #142 (factory-shared anchor;
   API landed same-session) and #145 (coupled-assembly contamination
   under stratum heterogeneity; doc caveat landed, centering fix is
   v2.1). The tau-binding column and the SE-explosion accounting only
   exist because of per-rep anchoring (#142) and the #140 validity
   discipline -- the protocol findings were load-bearing.
5. *The owner-approved columns*: (a) tau-binding: above. (b) CU+LM
   robustness: convergence degrades gracefully under misspecification
   (0.994 -> 0.970) while median step counts quadruple; no evidence
   motivating a trust-region option on the flat path (#9's quotient
   half untouched by design).

* Decision briefs (owner decisions; evidence above)

** #134 --- emergency PD floor for CU mid-solve Cholesky failure

The floor's actual targets, measured: exactly-singular zero-diagonal
V rows (~0.3% of reps at the G=2/p_w=0.5 boundary --- the mask-only
readout's named event) and the mid-solve failures inside the coupled
design-aware arms (10-19% exclusions, concentrated in the #145
pathology). Against: (i) the regime doing the most inferential damage
(feasible-but-degraded, J rej 0.31) is invisible to ANY
infeasibility-keyed floor by construction; (ii) the reps a floor would
rescue are exactly those whose V is least trustworthy --- the floor
converts loud, counted failures (#138/#140 discipline) into quiet fits
with dubious inference; (iii) the largest consumer of failures (#145)
is a covariance defect with its own fix path. /Recommendation: close
with reason --- the diagnose-loudly policy is the better contract, the
floor is cheap to revive if a consumer demands point estimates under
pathological V, and the evidence shows it would not address the
regime that matters./

** Davies/Imhof exact generalised chi-squared (Arc-3's last decision)

In the regime Theorem 6 actually describes (fixed-tau binding from
near-collinearity), Welch--Satterthwaite brings the J p-value from a
failing KS 0.211 to a passing 0.085 over the full law. The cases where
the adjusted p-value stays miscalibrated are the #145-contaminated
arms, where the covariance itself is wrong and exactness of the
reference distribution is beside the point. /Recommendation: close as
not-needed --- W-S calibrates where the limit theory applies; Davies/
Imhof would buy exactness in a regime exhibiting no deficit./

** Run-protocol incidents (recorded; they are findings, not noise)

1. The first full run's =design_aware= study was OOM-killed at 9.4 GB:
   the then-unfixed #139 append-only caches retained ~14 MB per bare
   =estimate()= call on the per-rep-anchoring path. Fixed in-package
   (#139), mitigated in-harness.
2. The mitigation (=jax.clear_caches()= per replicate) traded the leak
   for a ~3-5x wall-clock recompilation tax (XLA kernel compilations
   ARE re-hit across reps even though closure traces are not):
   measured ~12 s/rep at N = 2000 vs the ~2.6 s/rep pilot rate. The
   second run's =design_aware= study ran under per-rep clearing
   (correct, slow: ~6 h); =ridge_binding= and the shipped harness use
   every-25 clearing (~350 MB swing, few-percent overhead). Clearing
   frequency has zero numerical effect; the studies' statistics are
   unaffected.
3. design_aware's sidecar records the worktree commit current at its
   END (the every-25 commit landed mid-study); the code it RAN was the
   per-rep-clearing revision one commit earlier. Timing-only drift;
   noted here so the sidecar is read correctly.
4. The per-rep-anchored studies run n_reps = 300 (not 500): MC SE on a
   5% rate is 1.3%, and the binding-event counts (>= 80 per binding
   arm) clear the jadj gate comfortably; the factory studies run 500.
