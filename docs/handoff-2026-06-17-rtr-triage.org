** #159 (maintainer, RESOLVED): sigma->0 boundary is a CONSUMER-MODEL issue
- emu-gmm's CUE is exact / scale-invariant (verified bit-identical to moment
  scale 1e15); tau=0 at the boundary. NOT an estimator/regulariser bug.
- Root cause: the consumer model's explicit holder-mean demeaning
  ``(m**sigma - mbar)/|sigma|`` -- as sigma->0 it removes the common scale
  before the estimator (CUE self-normalisation becomes a no-op) AND subtracts
  at the 1/|sigma| scale (catastrophic cancellation that corrupts the moment
  DIRECTION, which CUE cannot fix). One-line fix: ``m**sigma -> expm1(sigma*log
  m)`` everywhere incl. the holder-mean (the 1's cancel analytically) -- stable
  to 1e-13, economics/composition bit-identical. Filed downstream against
  ../Seasonality (= task C; the consumer owns it). This repo's design realdata
  test (TestDesignSpec) re-pins to the stable limit J~7.087/p~0.792 -- ALSO the
  consumer's to land once the stable moment is adopted.
